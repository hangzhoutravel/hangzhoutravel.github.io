Hangzhou Quarantine Rules: What Travelers Must Prepare

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Traveling to Hangzhou, the picturesque capital of Zhejiang Province, is a dream for many. Known for its serene West Lake, ancient tea culture, and cutting-edge tech scene, the city seamlessly blends tradition with modernity. However, as global travel adapts to post-pandemic norms, understanding Hangzhou’s quarantine rules remains crucial for a smooth journey. Whether you’re visiting for business, leisure, or the upcoming Asian Games, here’s everything you need to know before packing your bags.

Current Quarantine Policies for International Arrivals

Hangzhou follows China’s dynamic "Zero-COVID" strategy, which means quarantine rules can change based on global and domestic infection rates. As of mid-2024, here’s what travelers should expect:

1. Pre-Departure Requirements

Before boarding your flight, ensure you’ve completed these steps:
- Negative PCR Test: A test taken within 48 hours of departure is mandatory. Some airlines may require an additional rapid antigen test.
- Health Declaration Form: Submit via the China Customs app or WeChat Mini Program.
- Vaccination Proof: While not always required, being fully vaccinated can streamline entry.

2. Quarantine Duration

  • Standard Protocol: Most travelers undergo 7 days of centralized quarantine at a government-designated facility, followed by 3 days of home monitoring.
  • Exceptions: Rules may vary for travelers from high-risk countries or those with special permits (e.g., athletes for the Asian Games).

3. Post-Quarantine Testing

Even after quarantine, you’ll need to take PCR tests on Days 1, 3, and 5 of home monitoring. Local community staff will guide you on testing locations.

Essential Documents and Apps

Must-Have Travel Apps

  • Alipay or WeChat Pay: For contactless payments and health code integration.
  • Zhejiang Health Code (健康码): A green code is required to enter public spaces.
  • 行程码 (Travel Code): Tracks your 14-day travel history.

Physical Documents

  • Printed copies of your PCR test results and hotel reservations.
  • Emergency contacts, including your country’s consulate in Hangzhou.

What to Pack for Quarantine

Packing wisely can make quarantine more comfortable:
- Comfortable Clothing: Light layers for Hangzhou’s humid climate.
- Entertainment: Books, downloaded shows, or a VPN (access to global sites may be restricted).
- Snacks: Quarantine meals are provided, but favorite treats help morale.
- Medications: Prescription drugs with English/Chinese labels.

Navigating Hangzhou Post-Quarantine

Once cleared, explore these highlights while staying mindful of local health rules:

Top Attractions

  • West Lake (西湖): Rent a bike or take a boat cruise.
  • Lingyin Temple: A sacred Buddhist site with stunning carvings.
  • Xixi Wetland Park: A tranquil escape from the city.

Dining and Shopping

  • Hefang Street: For traditional snacks like dongpo pork and silk souvenirs.
  • In77 Mall: A hub for luxury brands and tech gadgets.

Transport Tips

  • Use Didi (China’s Uber) or the metro (mask required).
  • Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:30 AM and 5–7 PM).

Special Considerations for Events

2024 Asian Games

If attending the Games (September–October 2024), check for event-specific protocols:
- Fast-Track Entry: Athletes and officials may have shortened quarantine.
- Venue Access: Proof of testing within 24 hours is likely required.

Staying Updated

Rules can shift quickly. Monitor these resources:
- Embassy Alerts: Your home country’s consulate in Shanghai/Hangzhou.
- Official Channels: Hangzhou government WeChat accounts or the China CDC website.

By preparing thoroughly, you’ll spend less time stressing over logistics and more time enjoying Hangzhou’s timeless beauty. Safe travels!
