10 Best Places to See Fireflies in Hangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

There’s something magical about watching fireflies light up the night like tiny, floating stars. Hangzhou, with its lush landscapes and serene waterways, offers some of the best spots to witness this enchanting natural phenomenon. Whether you're a nature lover, a photographer, or simply looking for a romantic evening, these locations promise an unforgettable experience. Here are the 10 best places to see fireflies in Hangzhou.

1. Xixi Wetland Park

Why It’s Special

Xixi Wetland Park is one of Hangzhou’s most famous ecological reserves, and it transforms into a firefly paradise during summer evenings. The dense vegetation and calm waters create the perfect habitat for these glowing insects.

Best Time to Visit

Late June to early August, just after sunset.

Pro Tip

Take a boat ride through the wetlands for an up-close view of the fireflies dancing over the water.

2. Longjing Tea Plantations

Why It’s Special

The terraced hills of Longjing are not just for tea lovers—fireflies thrive here too! The lack of light pollution and the organic farming practices make this a prime spot for firefly sightings.

Best Time to Visit

Mid-July, when the tea harvest season winds down and the nights are warm.

Pro Tip

Pair your firefly-watching with a cup of freshly brewed Longjing tea at a nearby teahouse.

3. Hangzhou Botanical Garden

Why It’s Special

This garden is a hidden gem for firefly enthusiasts. The diverse plant life and shaded pathways create an ideal environment for fireflies to flourish.

Best Time to Visit

Early summer evenings, around 7:30–9:00 PM.

Pro Tip

Bring a picnic and enjoy the fireflies under the stars—just remember to avoid bright lights that might disturb them.

4. Yunqi Bamboo Forest

Why It’s Special

The towering bamboo groves of Yunqi provide a mystical backdrop for fireflies. The cool, humid air here keeps the fireflies active well into the night.

Best Time to Visit

Late July, when the bamboo forest is at its lushest.

Pro Tip

Wear comfortable shoes—the forest trails can be slippery after rain.

5. West Lake (Xi Hu) at Dusk

Why It’s Special

While West Lake is famous for its daytime beauty, few know that its quieter corners come alive with fireflies after dark. The lakeside reeds and willow trees are particularly popular spots.

Best Time to Visit

June to August, especially on moonless nights.

Pro Tip

Head to the less crowded northern shores for a more intimate experience.

6. Meijiawu Tea Village

Why It’s Special

Another tea-growing area, Meijiawu offers a tranquil setting for firefly watching. The fireflies here often gather near small streams and tea bushes.

Best Time to Visit

Early July, when the evenings are warm but not too humid.

Pro Tip

Stay overnight in a local guesthouse to fully immerse yourself in the village’s peaceful atmosphere.

7. Hangzhou Safari Park

Why It’s Special

This might seem unexpected, but the park’s wooded areas and natural habitats are home to thriving firefly populations. The park occasionally hosts special nighttime events for visitors.

Best Time to Visit

Check the park’s schedule for summer night tours.

Pro Tip

Bring a zoom lens for your camera—fireflies here are used to human presence and may come closer.

8. Qiantang River Banks

Why It’s Special

The quieter stretches of the Qiantang River, away from the city lights, are fantastic for spotting fireflies. The river breeze adds to the magical ambiance.

Best Time to Visit

Late June to early August, especially after a rainy day.

Pro Tip

Combine your visit with a stroll along the riverbank to enjoy the cool evening air.

9. Hangzhou Children’s Park

Why It’s Special

This park isn’t just for kids—its wooded areas and flower gardens attract fireflies in droves during summer. The park’s playful atmosphere makes it a fun spot for families.

Best Time to Visit

Weekday evenings in July, when the park is less crowded.

Pro Tip

Bring a small red flashlight (fireflies are less sensitive to red light) to navigate without disturbing them.

10. Tianzhu Mountain Area

Why It’s Special

For those willing to venture a bit farther, the Tianzhu Mountain area offers a secluded, almost fairy-tale-like setting for firefly watching. The mountain streams and dense foliage create a perfect habitat.

Best Time to Visit

Mid to late July, when the mountain temperatures are just right.

Pro Tip

Hike up to one of the mountain’s viewing platforms for a panoramic view of fireflies dotting the valley below.

Each of these spots offers a unique way to experience the wonder of fireflies in Hangzhou. Whether you prefer the tranquility of a bamboo forest or the lively atmosphere of a lakeside park, there’s a perfect location waiting for you. Pack your camera, leave the bright lights behind, and let nature’s tiny lanterns guide your way.
