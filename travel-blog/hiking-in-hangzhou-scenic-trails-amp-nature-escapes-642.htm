Hiking in Hangzhou: Scenic Trails & Nature Escapes

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nestled between misty mountains and serene lakes, Hangzhou is a hiker’s paradise. While the city is famous for its UNESCO-listed West Lake, the surrounding trails offer breathtaking landscapes, ancient tea plantations, and hidden temples. Whether you’re a casual walker or an avid trekker, Hangzhou’s trails promise unforgettable adventures. Here’s your guide to the best routes, tips, and hidden gems.

The Best Hiking Trails in Hangzhou

1. Dragon Well Village (Longjing) Tea Plantation Trail

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
Distance: 5-8 km (depending on route)

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without exploring the emerald-green tea fields of Longjing. This trail winds through terraced plantations where China’s most famous green tea is grown. Start at Longjing Village and follow the stone paths uphill for panoramic views. Stop by a local farmhouse for a freshly brewed cup of Longjing tea—the perfect mid-hike refreshment.

Pro Tip: Visit in spring (March-April) to witness tea-picking season, when the fields buzz with activity.

2. Fei Lai Feng (Peak Flown From Afar) & Lingyin Temple

Difficulty: Moderate
Distance: 4-6 km loop

Combine culture and nature on this trail near Lingyin Temple, one of Hangzhou’s most revered Buddhist sites. The hike up Fei Lai Feng is dotted with ancient rock carvings and caves. At the summit, you’ll be rewarded with sweeping views of the temple complex and surrounding forests.

Don’t Miss: The 1,000-year-old grotto statues along the path—some of Hangzhou’s finest Buddhist art.

3. Jiuxi Eighteen Streams (Jiuxi Yanshu)

Difficulty: Easy
Distance: 6 km one-way

A shaded, creek-side trail perfect for hot summer days. Follow the babbling streams through bamboo groves and past quaint footbridges. The path ends near Meijiawu Tea Village, another great spot for tea tasting.

Why It’s Special: Less crowded than West Lake, offering a peaceful retreat into nature.

4. Five Clouds Mountain (Wuyun Shan)

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Distance: 10-12 km loop

For a full-day adventure, tackle Five Clouds Mountain. The trail passes through dense forests, historic temples (like Zhenji Temple), and offers multiple viewpoints. The highest peak, Tianzhu Mountain, is a favorite among photographers for its sunrise vistas.

Local Secret: Pack a picnic and rest at one of the mountain’s hidden clearings—ideal for a quiet lunch.

5. Xixi Wetland Park Trails

Difficulty: Easy
Distance: 3-5 km (various routes)

While not a traditional hike, Xixi’s wooden boardwalks and waterways provide a unique "urban wetland" experience. Rent a paddleboat or stroll through reedy marshes, spotting herons and traditional water villages.

Best Time to Go: Autumn, when the reeds turn golden and the air is crisp.

Essential Hiking Tips for Hangzhou

When to Hike

  • Spring (March-May): Ideal for tea plantation trails; mild temperatures and blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September-November): Cool weather, fewer crowds, and stunning foliage.
  • Avoid summer midday heat—start early or opt for shaded routes like Jiuxi.

What to Pack

  • Sturdy walking shoes (some trails have uneven stone steps).
  • Light rain gear (Hangzhou’s weather can change quickly).
  • Water and snacks (though tea villages offer refreshments).
  • A camera—you’ll want to capture the landscapes!

Trail Etiquette

  • Respect tea plantations; stay on marked paths.
  • Carry out trash (many trails lack bins).
  • Greet locals with a friendly "Nihao"—they might share insider tips!

Beyond Hiking: Hangzhou’s Nature Escapes

Bamboo Rafting on Fuchun River

Just outside Hangzhou, this tranquil activity lets you drift past karst cliffs and rural scenery. Pair it with a visit to nearby ancient towns like Wuzhen.

Camping at Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

A 2-hour drive from Hangzhou, this sprawling lake has islands perfect for overnight camping. Rent a tent or stay in a lakeside cabin.

Cycling Around West Lake

Prefer wheels to boots? Rent a bike and circle West Lake’s 15-km perimeter, stopping at pagodas and lakeside cafes.

Final Thoughts

Hangzhou’s trails are more than just walks—they’re journeys through history, culture, and some of China’s most iconic landscapes. Whether sipping tea atop Longjing or tracing Buddhist carvings on Fei Lai Feng, every step reveals a new wonder. So lace up your boots, breathe in the fresh mountain air, and let Hangzhou’s nature work its magic.

Happy hiking!
