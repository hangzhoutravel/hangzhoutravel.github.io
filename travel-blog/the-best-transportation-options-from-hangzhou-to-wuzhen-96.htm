The Best Transportation Options from Hangzhou to Wuzhen

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Wuzhen, one of China's most iconic water towns, is a must-visit destination for travelers exploring the Zhejiang province. Located approximately 80 kilometers northeast of Hangzhou, Wuzhen is renowned for its picturesque canals, traditional architecture, and cultural charm. If you're planning a trip from Hangzhou to Wuzhen, here’s a comprehensive guide to the best transportation options.

Taking a Direct Bus from Hangzhou to Wuzhen

Where to Catch the Bus

The most straightforward way to get to Wuzhen is by taking a direct bus. You can catch these buses from the Hangzhou Passenger Transport Central Station (Hangzhou Zhongxin Qichezhan). This station is conveniently located and accessible by metro and taxi.

Ticket Booking

You can purchase tickets at the station on the day of travel, but it’s recommended to book in advance, especially during weekends and holidays. Tickets are typically available online through platforms like Ctrip or directly at the station counter.

Travel Time and Cost

The bus ride takes approximately 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions. Tickets usually cost around 30-40 RMB, making it an affordable choice for budget-conscious travelers.

Advantages

  • Direct Route: No need to transfer, ensuring a hassle-free journey.
  • Comfort: Most buses are equipped with air conditioning and comfortable seating.
  • Cost-Effective: Ideal for travelers looking to save money.

Disadvantages

  • Limited Schedule: Buses may not operate late in the evening, so plan your trip accordingly.

Traveling by High-Speed Train and Shuttle Bus

High-Speed Train to Tongxiang

Wuzhen does not have a train station, but the nearest station, Tongxiang Railway Station, is well-connected to Hangzhou by high-speed rail. Trains depart from Hangzhou East Railway Station (Hangzhou Dongzhan) frequently throughout the day.

Booking Tickets

Train tickets can be booked online via platforms like 12306 or Ctrip. Ensure you select Tongxiang as your destination. Ticket prices range from 20-30 RMB, and the journey takes about 20 minutes.

From Tongxiang to Wuzhen

Upon arrival at Tongxiang Railway Station, take a shuttle bus to Wuzhen. These buses are conveniently located outside the station and operate frequently. The shuttle ride takes about 40 minutes and costs approximately 10 RMB.

Advantages

  • Speed: High-speed trains are the fastest option to reach Tongxiang.
  • Convenience: Frequent train and shuttle schedules make this a flexible choice.

Disadvantages

  • Transfers Required: This method involves switching between train and shuttle bus, which may be inconvenient for travelers with heavy luggage.

Driving from Hangzhou to Wuzhen

Renting a Car

If you prefer the freedom of driving, renting a car is a great option. Car rental services are available at Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport and within the city.

Route Details

The drive from Hangzhou to Wuzhen takes about 1.5 hours via the G60 or G25 expressways. The roads are well-maintained and offer scenic views along the way.

Advantages

  • Flexibility: Stop at interesting sights or detour to nearby attractions.
  • Convenience: Perfect for groups or families traveling together.

Disadvantages

  • Cost: Rental fees, tolls, and fuel costs may add up.
  • Parking: Limited parking options in Wuzhen can be a challenge.

Joining a Guided Tour

Tour Packages

Several travel agencies in Hangzhou offer day trips or overnight tours to Wuzhen. These packages typically include transportation, entry tickets, and a guided tour of the town.

Booking

Tours can be booked online, through your hotel, or at local travel agencies. Prices vary based on the inclusions and duration of the trip.

Advantages

  • All-Inclusive: Transportation, tickets, and a guide are taken care of.
  • Informative: Guides provide insights into Wuzhen’s history and culture.

Disadvantages

  • Less Flexibility: Fixed itineraries may not suit independent travelers.

Tips for a Smooth Journey

Best Time to Visit

The ideal seasons to visit Wuzhen are spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during Chinese holidays to steer clear of crowds.

Packing Essentials

  • Comfortable Shoes: Wuzhen’s cobblestone streets require sturdy footwear.
  • Rain Gear: An umbrella or raincoat is handy, especially during the rainy season.
  • Camera: Don’t miss capturing Wuzhen’s stunning scenery.

Entry Tickets

Wuzhen is divided into two main areas: the East Scenic Zone (Dongzha) and the West Scenic Zone (Xizha). Purchase tickets in advance to avoid long queues at the entrance. Combined tickets for both zones are available.

Language Barrier

While some staff and locals may speak basic English, having translation apps or knowing a few Mandarin phrases can enhance your experience.

Staying Overnight

For those looking to immerse themselves in Wuzhen’s charm, staying overnight in a local guesthouse or boutique hotel in Xizha is highly recommended. Many accommodations offer serene views of the canals and a chance to experience the town after the day-trippers leave.
