The Ultimate Hangzhou Bucket List: Best Things to Do

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China’s most enchanting cities. Known for its poetic landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned explorer, this ultimate Hangzhou bucket list will guide you through the best experiences the city has to offer.

1. Explore the Legendary West Lake (Xi Hu)

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without visiting West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This iconic lake has inspired poets, artists, and emperors for centuries.

Must-Do Activities at West Lake

  • Take a Leifeng Pagoda Sunset Tour – Climb to the top for breathtaking panoramic views.
  • Stroll Along Su Causeway (Su Di) – A scenic walkway lined with willow trees and lotus flowers.
  • Rent a Traditional Boat – Glide across the lake while sipping Longjing tea.
  • Watch the "Impression West Lake" Show – A mesmerizing outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou.

2. Sip Authentic Longjing Tea in Meijiawu

Hangzhou is famous for Longjing tea (Dragon Well tea), one of China’s most prized green teas. The Meijiawu Tea Village offers an immersive tea culture experience.

How to Enjoy Longjing Tea Like a Local

  • Visit a Tea Plantation – Learn about tea-picking and processing.
  • Join a Tea Ceremony – Discover the art of brewing the perfect cup.
  • Buy Fresh Tea Leaves – Take home some of the finest Longjing tea as a souvenir.

3. Wander Through Hefang Street (Qinghefang Ancient Street)

Step back in time at Hefang Street, a lively pedestrian street filled with traditional architecture, street food, and handicrafts.

Top Things to Do on Hefang Street

  • Try Hangzhou Snacks – Don’t miss Dongpo Pork and Beggar’s Chicken.
  • Visit Traditional Medicine Shops – Explore centuries-old herbal remedies.
  • Watch Silk Weaving Demonstrations – Hangzhou is renowned for its silk production.

4. Discover the Serenity of Lingyin Temple

Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat) is one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples, nestled at the foot of Feilai Feng (Peak Flown From Afar).

Highlights of Lingyin Temple

  • Marvel at the 1,000-Year-Old Rock Carvings – Intricate Buddhist statues line the cliffs.
  • Light Incense for Blessings – A spiritual experience for visitors.
  • Hike to the Nearby Tea Fields – Combine culture with nature.

5. Experience the Grand Canal by Night

The Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal, the world’s longest artificial waterway, comes alive at night with illuminated bridges and boat tours.

Best Ways to Enjoy the Grand Canal

  • Take a Night Cruise – Admire the dazzling reflections on the water.
  • Walk Along the Historic Canal Streets – Explore boutique shops and cafés.
  • Visit the China Umbrella Museum – A quirky yet fascinating stop.

6. Hike the Bamboo-Filled Trails of Yunqi Bamboo Path

For nature lovers, Yunqi Bamboo Path offers a peaceful escape with towering bamboo groves and tranquil streams.

What to Expect on the Yunqi Hike

  • Walk the "Five Clouds Mountain" Trail – A moderate hike with scenic viewpoints.
  • Visit Yunqi Temple – A hidden gem surrounded by greenery.
  • Photograph the Bamboo Forest – A dreamy setting straight out of a painting.

7. Indulge in Hangzhou’s Famous Dishes

Hangzhou’s cuisine is a highlight of any visit. From delicate dim sum to hearty stews, the city’s food scene is unforgettable.

Must-Try Hangzhou Dishes

  • West Lake Vinegar Fish (Xi Hu Cu Yu) – Sweet, sour, and tender.
  • Shrimp with Longjing Tea (Longjing Xia Ren) – A unique tea-infused delicacy.
  • Sister Song’s Fish Broth (Song Sao Yu Geng) – A comforting local favorite.

8. Visit the National Silk Museum

As the "Silk Capital of China," Hangzhou’s National Silk Museum is a treasure trove of textile history.

Exhibits Not to Miss

  • Ancient Silk Road Artifacts – Discover how silk shaped global trade.
  • Live Silkworm Demonstrations – See how silk is made from cocoons.
  • Fashion Through the Dynasties – Stunning historical garments on display.

9. Cycle Around Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

Just a short trip from Hangzhou, Qiandao Lake is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Activities at Qiandao Lake

  • Rent a Bike and Explore – Scenic lakeside cycling routes.
  • Visit Bird Island and Monkey Island – Wildlife encounters.
  • Try Local Organic Fish Dishes – Freshly caught from the lake.

10. Relax at Xixi Wetland Park

A hidden oasis, Xixi Wetland Park is a vast network of waterways, pagodas, and lush greenery.

How to Enjoy Xixi Wetland

  • Take a Gondola Ride – Drift through serene canals.
  • Visit Ancient Water Towns – Preserved traditional houses.
  • Photograph Lotus Flowers in Summer – A photographer’s paradise.

Hangzhou is a city where ancient traditions meet modern charm. Whether you’re sipping tea by West Lake, exploring ancient streets, or hiking through bamboo forests, every moment here feels like a scene from a classic Chinese poem. Pack your bags and start ticking off this ultimate Hangzhou bucket list!
