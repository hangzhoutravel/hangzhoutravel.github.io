Packing for Hangzhou’s Best Rooftop Bars

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, a city where ancient charm meets modern sophistication, is home to some of China’s most breathtaking rooftop bars. Whether you’re sipping a craft cocktail overlooking West Lake or dancing under the stars with the city skyline as your backdrop, packing the right essentials can elevate your experience. Here’s your ultimate guide to looking stylish, staying comfortable, and making the most of Hangzhou’s vibrant rooftop scene.

The Essentials: What to Bring

1. Weather-Appropriate Clothing

Hangzhou’s weather can be unpredictable, especially during the humid summers or chilly winters. For summer evenings, lightweight fabrics like linen or silk will keep you cool, while a stylish shawl or light jacket is perfect for breezy nights. In cooler months, layering is key—think a chic trench coat or a tailored blazer.

Pro tip: Avoid overly casual outfits like flip-flops or gym wear. Most rooftop bars in Hangzhou have a smart-casual dress code.

2. Camera or Smartphone with a Good Lens

The views from Hangzhou’s rooftop bars are Instagram-worthy. Whether it’s the golden hues of sunset over West Lake or the glittering city lights, you’ll want to capture the moment. Pack a portable charger to ensure your phone doesn’t die mid-snap.

3. Compact Umbrella or Foldable Raincoat

Hangzhou is known for its sudden rain showers, especially during the plum rain season (June-July). A sleek, compact umbrella won’t cramp your style but will save you from an unexpected downpour.

4. Comfortable Yet Stylish Footwear

Rooftop bars often involve stairs, elevators, and sometimes uneven surfaces. Opt for fashionable yet comfortable shoes—block heels, loafers, or designer sneakers are great choices.

Must-Have Accessories

1. Sunglasses for Day-to-Night Transitions

If you’re planning a sunset session, a pair of polarized sunglasses will protect your eyes while adding a touch of glam. Transition lenses are a bonus for those who wear prescription glasses.

2. A Small Crossbody Bag or Clutch

Keep your essentials (phone, lipstick, ID, and credit cards) secure without lugging around a bulky purse. Many rooftop bars have limited seating, so a hands-free bag is ideal.

3. Portable Fan or Cooling Mist

Summer nights in Hangzhou can get sticky. A handheld fan or a refreshing facial mist will keep you cool without ruining your makeup.

Drinks and Snacks: What to Expect

Hangzhou’s rooftop bars are known for their creative cocktails and local twists. Here’s what you might encounter:

1. Longjing Tea-Infused Cocktails

A nod to Hangzhou’s famous Longjing tea, many bars incorporate this delicate flavor into their drinks. Try a Longjing Gin Fizz or a Dragon Well Mojito for a taste of local tradition.

2. Craft Beers with a View

Places like Xanadu Rooftop Bar often feature locally brewed craft beers. Pair yours with small bites like spicy lotus root chips or crispy tofu.

3. Late-Night Bites

Some bars offer light snacks, but if you’re planning a long night, research nearby late-night eateries. Hangzhou’s Hefang Street is a great spot for post-bar munchies.

Pro Tips for a Seamless Experience

1. Make Reservations in Advance

Popular spots like The Lounge at Amanfayun or Cloud 9 at the Hyatt fill up quickly, especially on weekends. Book a table in advance to secure the best views.

2. Check for Special Events

Many rooftop bars host themed nights, live music, or DJ sets. Follow their social media pages to stay updated.

3. Learn a Few Mandarin Phrases

While many staff speak English, knowing basic phrases like “Zhè ge shì shénme?” (What is this?) or “Zài lái yī bēi” (One more, please) can enhance your experience.

4. Respect Local Etiquette

Tipping isn’t customary in China, but a smile and polite demeanor go a long way. Avoid loud conversations or disruptive behavior—Hangzhou’s rooftop bars often emphasize a relaxed, elegant vibe.

Final Packing Checklist

Before you head out, run through this quick list:
- Weather-appropriate outfit
- Camera or smartphone + charger
- Compact umbrella
- Comfortable shoes
- Sunglasses
- Small bag for essentials
- Portable fan (summer) or light scarf (winter)
- Cash or mobile payment apps (Alipay/WeChat Pay)

Now you’re ready to conquer Hangzhou’s rooftop bar scene in style. Whether you’re toasting to a special occasion or simply soaking in the city’s beauty, the right preparation will ensure an unforgettable night.
