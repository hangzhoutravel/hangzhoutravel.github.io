Best Photography Spots in Hangzhou for Photography Enthusiasts

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, known as one of China’s most picturesque cities, offers a perfect blend of natural landscapes, classical architecture, and modern urban scenery. Whether you're interested in capturing serene lake views, ancient temples, lush mountains, or bustling city streets, Hangzhou provides endless opportunities for photographers. From golden sunrise reflections on West Lake to misty tea plantations in Longjing, this city is a dream destination for anyone passionate about photography.

West Lake: The Iconic Scenic Wonderland

West Lake (Xī Hú) is undoubtedly the most famous photography spot in Hangzhou. With its tranquil waters, arched stone bridges, and historic pagodas, it offers countless perspectives for photographers.

1. Broken Bridge (Duanqiao) in Winter

  • One of the most legendary spots on West Lake, famous for the romantic tale of the White Snake.
  • In winter, when frost or snow covers the bridge, it creates a dreamlike scenery, perfect for unique compositions.
  • Early mornings provide the best lighting, with mist rising from the lake.

2. Leifeng Pagoda at Sunset

  • One of the best sunset photography locations in Hangzhou.
  • The pagoda’s golden glow reflects beautifully on the lake, creating a stunning silhouette effect.
  • Position your camera across the lake for a panoramic view of the pagoda against the West Lake skyline.

3. Three Pools Mirroring the Moon (Sān Tán Yìn Yuè)

  • This famous site features three stone pagodas emerging from the water, originally designed to hold candles.
  • Best photographed at night when the lights reflect on the water.
  • Taking a boat ride allows for a closer perspective and a more dramatic composition.

4. Su Causeway (Sū Dī) in Spring

  • A must-visit during cherry blossom season when the entire causeway is lined with pink and white flowers.
  • Capturing the reflections of the trees in the water adds depth and symmetry to your shots.

Longjing Tea Plantations: Lush Green Terraces

Longjing (Dragon Well) Tea Plantations offer a refreshing change from the lake scenery. Located in the rolling hills southwest of Hangzhou, this area provides incredible textures and layers for landscape photography.

1. Meijiawu Tea Village

  • One of the most picturesque tea fields, with neatly arranged rows of tea bushes.
  • Best visited in early morning or late afternoon when the sunlight casts long shadows over the fields.
  • Farmers picking tea leaves add a great human element to your shots.

2. Longjing Village

  • A less touristy alternative with winding stone paths leading through the tea fields.
  • Ideal for drone photography, capturing the geometric patterns of the tea terraces.

Nine Creeks in Misty Forest: A Hidden Paradise

Jiǔ Xī Yán Shū is a lesser-known gem for nature photographers. This area features a meandering creek, wooden bridges, and dense bamboo forests.

  • Best shot in the early morning when mist lingers over the water, creating a mystical atmosphere.
  • Slow shutter speed techniques work well to capture the movement of the flowing creek.
  • In autumn, golden leaves contrast beautifully with the emerald green bamboo.

Xixi National Wetland Park: A Water Village in Nature

Xīxī Wetlands, located west of West Lake, is a peaceful area filled with winding waterways, ancient houses, and lotus ponds.

  • The best time for photography is during sunrise or sunset when the water reflects the warm sky colors.
  • Traditional boats floating on the river make for great compositions.
  • In summer, lotus flowers bloom across the water, adding a splash of color.

Qinghefang Ancient Street: Capturing Old Hangzhou

For street and documentary photography, Qinghefang Ancient Street offers vibrant scenes of old Hangzhou. This historical street is lined with Ming and Qing dynasty buildings, traditional tea houses, and street performers.

  • Night photography is particularly rewarding here, with red lanterns illuminating the narrow alleys.
  • Using a fast lens helps capture the movement of people while keeping the historic details sharp.
  • Close-up shots of street vendors, calligraphy artists, and craftsmen create compelling storytelling images.

Chenghuang Pavilion: A Stunning Cityscape View

Perched on Wushan Hill, Chenghuang Pavilion provides one of the best panoramic views of Hangzhou.

  • At sunset, the city skyline blends beautifully with the traditional rooftops below.
  • Long exposure shots at night capture the city lights in all their glory.
  • The pavilion itself, with its intricate wooden carvings and golden roof, is a great subject for architectural photography.

Baochu Pagoda: Overlooking West Lake

For those seeking an elevated vantage point, Baochu Pagoda is a fantastic choice. Located on Precious Stone Hill, this slim, multi-tiered pagoda offers a breathtaking view of West Lake.

  • Morning hikes provide the best lighting conditions, with mist rolling over the lake.
  • Wide-angle lenses help capture both the pagoda and the surrounding landscape.
  • Autumn photography is particularly stunning, as the hill is covered in golden foliage.

The Grand Canal: Industrial and Cultural Contrasts

As one of the oldest and longest canals in the world, the Grand Canal in Hangzhou presents fascinating contrasts between old and new.

  • Boats passing through the canal provide dynamic elements for action shots.
  • The Gongchen Bridge, an ancient stone bridge, is a great foreground for cityscape compositions.
  • Night photography along the illuminated waterfront creates beautiful reflections.

Yangmeiling: The Secret Bamboo Forest

For those who love nature and minimalist photography, Yangmeiling Bamboo Forest is a hidden gem.

  • A perfect location for capturing leading lines as the tall bamboo stalks create a natural tunnel effect.
  • Misty mornings enhance the mystical feeling of the forest.
  • Black-and-white photography works exceptionally well here, emphasizing the contrast between the bamboo trunks and the soft light filtering through the leaves.

Xianghu Lake: The Lesser-Known Alternative to West Lake

For photographers who want to escape the crowds, Xianghu Lake offers similar scenic beauty with fewer tourists.

  • The best times for photography are sunrise and sunset when the lake reflects the sky’s changing colors.
  • Small fishing boats add traditional elements to the compositions.
  • Aerial photography works well to capture the full scale of the lake and its surrounding hills.

Hangzhou is a city filled with photographic inspiration, from its classical landscapes and historic streets to its serene tea fields and modern skyline. Whether you specialize in landscape, architecture, portrait, or street photography, Hangzhou’s diverse scenery ensures that every photographer will find something to capture.
