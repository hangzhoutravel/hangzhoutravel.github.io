Unique Handicrafts of Hangzhou You Must Take Home

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, a city renowned for its rich history, breathtaking landscapes, and vibrant culture, is also famous for its exceptional traditional handicrafts. Known as one of China’s cultural hubs, Hangzhou has a long history of artistic production that blends delicate techniques with rich local traditions. From silk and tea to intricate paper-cutting art and woodwork, the city offers an array of unique and beautiful handicrafts perfect for those looking to bring home a piece of its cultural heritage.

In this article, we will explore the most iconic handicrafts from Hangzhou, delving into their origins, significance, and where to find them. Whether you are a seasoned collector or a first-time visitor, Hangzhou’s handicrafts provide an opportunity to own something truly special, offering a glimpse into the city’s storied past and vibrant creative present.

1. Silk Products: A Taste of Hangzhou’s Legacy

Hangzhou has been a center for silk production for over a thousand years, earning the title of the "Silk Capital of China." The city’s rich silk heritage is deeply connected to its ancient silk trade, which flourished during the Tang and Song dynasties. As a result, Hangzhou has produced some of the finest silk fabrics, garments, and accessories in the world.

Visitors to Hangzhou can explore silk markets and factories, where they can observe the intricate process of silk production. A standout product is the Hangzhou silk quilt, renowned for its softness and lightness. Silk scarves, embroidered dresses, and traditional silk brocades are other popular items that make perfect souvenirs. You can find these exquisite silk items in places like Hangzhou Silk Museum and markets around West Lake, such as the Zhejiang Silk Market.

2. Longjing Tea and Tea Accessories

Hangzhou is not only famous for its natural beauty but also for its rich tea culture. The city is home to Longjing Tea (Dragon Well Tea), one of China’s most famous and prized green teas. The tea is grown in the picturesque Longjing Village, nestled in the hills near West Lake. The leaves are carefully hand-picked and processed using traditional methods passed down through generations, giving the tea a fresh, delicate flavor.

In addition to the tea itself, visitors can purchase beautifully crafted tea accessories, such as tea sets, teapots, and tea cups made from porcelain or clay. These items, often hand-painted with intricate designs, make for elegant and meaningful souvenirs. The Longjing tea farms around Hangzhou offer visitors the chance to try freshly brewed tea and purchase high-quality tea leaves directly from the source. Local tea shops around West Lake also carry a wide range of Longjing Tea and related accessories.

3. Hangzhou Paper-Cutting Art

Hangzhou’s paper-cutting art is a fine example of traditional Chinese folk art. Originating from the city’s rich cultural heritage, Hangzhou paper cuts are intricate designs made by carefully cutting patterns into paper. These patterns often depict scenes from nature, folklore, animals, and Chinese mythology. Some of the most common subjects include birds, flowers, and figures from traditional Chinese stories.

One of the best places to find Hangzhou paper-cutting art is at the Hangzhou Museum of Art and local markets, where skilled artisans create custom pieces right before your eyes. Visitors can purchase paper cuts in various forms, from small decorative pieces to larger, more intricate wall hangings. These art pieces are perfect for those looking for a unique and meaningful gift or a piece of Chinese culture to bring home.

4. Bamboo Products: The Craftsmanship of Nature

Hangzhou is located in a region abundant with bamboo forests, and as a result, bamboo craftsmanship plays a significant role in the city’s cultural heritage. The city produces a wide range of bamboo products, including bamboo baskets, utensils, furniture, and even bamboo paper. These items reflect the region’s deep connection to nature and sustainable living.

Some of the most popular bamboo products include bamboo mats, handcrafted bamboo fans, and bamboo home decor items. The artistry involved in shaping and weaving bamboo into functional items is a testament to the skill of Hangzhou artisans. Visitors can explore the Bamboo Culture Museum to learn more about the history of bamboo craftsmanship and shop for authentic bamboo items at local handicraft shops and markets.

5. Hangzhou Fans: Tradition in Motion

Fans have been an integral part of Chinese culture for centuries, and Hangzhou is no exception. The city is famous for its hand-painted silk fans and folding fans, which are often adorned with intricate designs depicting natural landscapes, mythical creatures, and traditional Chinese symbols.

Hangzhou fans are highly regarded for their fine craftsmanship and artistic quality. They are often handmade using delicate materials like silk, bamboo, and paper, and they are painted with exquisite designs that reflect Hangzhou’s natural beauty. Many of these fans are functional as well as decorative, making them perfect souvenirs. Visitors can find them at local stores around West Lake and local craft markets.

6. Chinese Calligraphy Brushes

Hangzhou has a long tradition of Chinese calligraphy, and the city is home to some of the finest calligraphy brushes in China. These brushes are made from high-quality materials such as wolf hair, goat hair, or synthetic fibers, and they are crafted with great precision by skilled artisans.

The brushes are available in a wide range of sizes and are often sold with ink stones and rice paper for creating traditional Chinese calligraphy art. Whether you’re an experienced calligrapher or a beginner, Hangzhou’s calligraphy brushes are a perfect souvenir for anyone interested in Chinese art and culture. You can purchase them from calligraphy supply stores and artisan workshops scattered throughout the city.

7. Hangzhou Porcelain: A Tradition of Beauty

Porcelain has been an important part of China’s cultural history for thousands of years, and Hangzhou has long been known for its porcelain production. The city’s porcelain items, particularly its delicate teacups, vases, plates, and figurines, are highly prized for their beauty and craftsmanship.

Hangzhou’s porcelain items are often decorated with traditional Chinese motifs, including flowers, dragons, phoenixes, and scenes from classical literature. These items are not only beautiful but also steeped in history, reflecting the artistic and cultural heritage of the region. The Hangzhou Porcelain Museum is a great place to explore the city’s porcelain history and purchase high-quality porcelain pieces directly from local artisans.

8. Wooden Carvings and Crafts

Hangzhou is also famous for its wooden carvings, a traditional art form that involves intricate designs carved into wood using sharp tools. These carvings often depict scenes from nature, animals, Chinese mythological figures, or traditional symbols of good luck and prosperity. The art of wooden carving in Hangzhou has been passed down through generations, and it remains a respected craft in the region.

One of the most famous types of wooden carvings is the West Lake wood carving, which often features images of the lake’s famous landscapes and historical sites. Visitors can find beautiful wooden carvings in the form of decorative panels, statues, and figurines at local shops and markets. These carvings are perfect for those looking for a unique and meaningful souvenir that reflects Hangzhou’s cultural heritage.

9. Silk Embroidery

In addition to silk fabric, Hangzhou is renowned for its silk embroidery. The city’s silk embroidery tradition dates back to the Tang Dynasty, and today it continues to be one of the most revered forms of art in Hangzhou. Local artisans create exquisite embroidered silk tapestries, clothing, and decorative pieces, often showcasing scenes of nature, animals, and famous Chinese landmarks.

The embroidery is often done by hand using colorful threads, and the designs are incredibly detailed and lifelike. You can find beautiful Hangzhou silk embroidery at local art galleries and shops. These intricate works of art make for stunning souvenirs and represent the high level of craftsmanship that Hangzhou is known for.

10. Hangzhou Lacquerware

Lacquerware is another traditional craft that has a long history in Hangzhou. The city is known for its beautifully crafted lacquered boxes, bowls, plates, and furniture. Lacquerware is typically made by applying multiple layers of lacquer to a wooden or bamboo base, creating a smooth, glossy finish that is both durable and attractive.

The designs on Hangzhou lacquerware often feature intricate patterns, including flowers, animals, and other traditional Chinese motifs. These items are often painted with bright, vivid colors, making them a striking addition to any home. Visitors can find a range of lacquerware items in shops around West Lake and local markets, where they are sold as decorative and functional pieces.

Hangzhou’s handicrafts reflect the city’s deep cultural roots and artistic traditions. Whether you’re looking for fine silk items, intricate porcelain, or handcrafted bamboo products, Hangzhou has something to offer for every collector. With a wide variety of handicrafts to choose from, you’re sure to find a meaningful and beautiful souvenir that captures the essence of this enchanting city.
