Hangzhou Travel Booking: Best Water Towns Nearby

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

When planning a trip to Hangzhou, most travelers focus on the iconic West Lake, ancient temples, and lush tea plantations. But just beyond the city lies a network of enchanting water towns that offer a glimpse into China’s traditional canal culture. These towns, with their stone bridges, wooden boats, and timeless charm, are perfect for a day trip or a weekend getaway. Here’s your guide to the best water towns near Hangzhou, complete with travel tips and must-see spots.

Why Visit Water Towns Near Hangzhou?

Hangzhou’s proximity to the Yangtze River Delta means it’s surrounded by some of China’s most picturesque water towns. These towns, often called "Venice of the East," feature:

  • Ancient architecture: Well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty buildings.
  • Canal networks: Narrow waterways lined with willow trees.
  • Local culture: Traditional crafts, street food, and folk performances.

Whether you’re a history buff, a photographer, or just looking for a peaceful escape, these towns deliver an unforgettable experience.

Top Water Towns to Explore

1. Wuzhen (乌镇)

Highlights:

  • Dongzha and Xizha Districts: Wuzhen is divided into two main areas—Dongzha (East Scenic Zone) is more commercial, while Xizha (West Scenic Zone) is quieter and ideal for overnight stays.
  • Ancient Workshops: Visit traditional indigo dyeing, woodcarving, and silk-spinning workshops.
  • Night Views: The town is beautifully lit after dark, with lanterns reflecting on the canals.

Travel Tips:

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn for mild weather.
  • How to Get There: A 1.5-hour drive from Hangzhou or a direct bus from Hangzhou East Station.

2. Xitang (西塘)

Highlights:

  • Covered Corridors (Langpeng): Sheltered walkways along the canals protect visitors from rain and sun.
  • Bar Street: A lively area with cafes and pubs blending traditional and modern vibes.
  • Sunrise Views: Arrive early to see mist rising over the water—a photographer’s dream.

Travel Tips:

  • Ticket Tip: Entry is free after 5 PM, but some attractions close.
  • Transport: Buses from Hangzhou take about 2 hours.

3. Nanxun (南浔)

Highlights:

  • European Influences: Unlike other water towns, Nanxun features a mix of Chinese and Western architecture.
  • Jiaye Library: A historic private library with rare collections.
  • Less Crowded: Ideal for travelers who prefer a quieter experience.

Travel Tips:

  • Food to Try: "Xunlian" (Nanxun lotus root starch cakes).
  • Getting There: 2-hour bus ride from Hangzhou.

How to Book Your Trip

Booking Tours vs. Independent Travel

Guided Tours:

  • Pros: Hassle-free, includes transportation and entry tickets.
  • Cons: Less flexibility in itinerary.

DIY Travel:

  • Pros: Freedom to explore at your own pace.
  • Cons: Requires more planning (transport, tickets, etc.).

Recommended Booking Platforms:

  • Ctrip (Trip.com): Reliable for train/bus tickets and tours.
  • Klook: Offers discounted combo deals for multiple attractions.
  • Local Agencies: Some guesthouses in Hangzhou arrange private tours.

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March–May): Pleasant weather, blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September–November): Cool temperatures, fewer tourists.
  • Avoid: National holidays (October 1–7) when crowds surge.

Packing Essentials

  • Comfortable Shoes: Cobblestone streets can be uneven.
  • Light Layers: Weather can change quickly.
  • Camera: Every corner is postcard-worthy.

Hidden Gems & Local Experiences

1. Early Morning Boat Rides

Beat the crowds by taking a wooden boat ride at sunrise—the water is calm, and the light is magical.

2. Tea Houses

Many water towns have quaint tea houses where you can sip Longjing tea while watching boats glide by.

3. Night Markets

Try local snacks like "stinky tofu" (chou doufu) or "sweet rice dumplings" (tangyuan).

Sustainable Travel Tips

  • Support Local: Buy handicrafts directly from artisans.
  • Reduce Waste: Carry a reusable water bottle (many towns have refill stations).
  • Respect Culture: Ask before photographing locals.

Final Thoughts

Hangzhou’s nearby water towns are more than just scenic spots—they’re living museums of China’s canal heritage. Whether you choose the bustling lanes of Wuzhen or the serene canals of Nanxun, each town offers a unique window into the past. Book your trip, pack your sense of adventure, and get ready to step into a world where time moves slower.
