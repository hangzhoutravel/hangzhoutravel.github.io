Ultimate Hangzhou Itinerary for 2024

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China’s most enchanting cities. Famous for its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers in 2024. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, this itinerary will help you make the most of your trip.

Day 1: Exploring West Lake & Historic Landmarks

Morning: West Lake Cruise

Start your day with a leisurely boat ride on West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Opt for a traditional wooden boat to glide across the water while taking in views of Leifeng Pagoda and Three Pools Mirroring the Moon. Don’t miss the Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao), especially if visiting in spring when peach blossoms bloom.

Afternoon: Lingyin Temple & Feilai Feng

Head to Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat), one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples. Wander through its grand halls before exploring Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar), a limestone grotto adorned with centuries-old carvings.

Evening: Hefang Street

Wrap up your day at Hefang Street, a bustling pedestrian lane filled with souvenir shops, street food, and traditional crafts. Try local snacks like Dongpo Pork and Osmanthus Cake.

Day 2: Tea Culture & Nature Escapes

Morning: Longjing Tea Plantations

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without visiting the Longjing (Dragon Well) Tea Plantations. Join a tea-picking tour, learn about tea processing, and sip freshly brewed Longjing tea—a favorite of emperors.

Afternoon: Nine Creeks & Eighteen Gullies

Hike through Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies (Jiu Xi Shi Ba Jian), a scenic trail flanked by bamboo forests and tea fields. The peaceful atmosphere makes it perfect for nature lovers.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

Cap off the night with "Impression West Lake", a breathtaking outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou. The show combines lights, music, and dance on the lake’s surface.

Day 3: Modern Hangzhou & Hidden Gems

Morning: China National Tea Museum

Dive deeper into tea culture at the China National Tea Museum. Interactive exhibits and tranquil gardens make it a relaxing stop.

Afternoon: Xixi Wetland Park

Explore Xixi Wetland Park, a lesser-known gem with waterways, ancient villages, and lush greenery. Take a gondola ride for the best views.

Evening: Qianjiang New Town Lights

For a modern twist, visit Qianjiang New Town at night. The skyline, illuminated by LED lights, rivals Shanghai’s Bund.

Pro Tips for Your Hangzhou Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) for blossoms or autumn (September-November) for mild weather.
  • Transport: Use the metro or DiDi (China’s Uber) for convenience.
  • Must-Try Dishes: Beggar’s Chicken, West Lake Vinegar Fish, and Sister Song’s Fish Broth.

Hangzhou seamlessly blends ancient charm with modern vibes, making it an unforgettable 2024 travel destination. Pack your bags and get ready for an adventure!
