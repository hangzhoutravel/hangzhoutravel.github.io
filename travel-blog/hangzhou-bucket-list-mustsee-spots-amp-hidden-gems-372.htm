Hangzhou Bucket List: Must-See Spots & Hidden Gems

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is a city where ancient charm meets modern sophistication. Famous for its poetic West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage, it’s no wonder Marco Polo once called it "the most splendid city in the world." Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this bucket list will guide you through Hangzhou’s must-see attractions and off-the-beaten-path treasures.

The Iconic Must-Sees

West Lake (Xi Hu)

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without experiencing the legendary West Lake. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this serene body of water is surrounded by pagodas, gardens, and willow-lined pathways.

Top Experiences:
- Boat Ride at Sunset: Glide across the lake on a traditional wooden boat for breathtaking views of Leifeng Pagoda glowing in the golden light.
- Walk Along Su Causeway: This scenic path, named after the poet Su Shi, is perfect for a leisurely stroll amid lotus flowers and arched bridges.
- Three Pools Mirroring the Moon: Visit this iconic islet, especially during the Mid-Autumn Festival when lanterns reflect magically on the water.

Lingyin Temple & Feilai Feng

One of China’s most significant Buddhist temples, Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat) dates back to 328 AD. The adjacent Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar) features hundreds of intricate stone carvings of Buddhist figures.

Pro Tip: Arrive early to avoid crowds and soak in the spiritual ambiance.

Hefang Street

Step back in time on this lively pedestrian street, where traditional architecture houses tea shops, silk vendors, and street food stalls.

Don’t Miss:
- Traditional Hangzhou snacks like Dongpo Pork and Beggar’s Chicken.
- Handmade crafts such as folding fans and bamboo scroll paintings.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

Longjing Tea Plantations

Venture beyond the tourist hubs to the rolling hills of Longjing Village, where some of China’s finest green tea is grown.

What to Do:
- Tea Picking Experience: Join locals during harvest season (March–April) to learn how Longjing tea is hand-plucked.
- Sip Tea at a Family-Owned Farm: Enjoy a freshly brewed cup while overlooking terraced fields.

Xixi Wetland Park

Often overshadowed by West Lake, this tranquil wetland is a paradise for nature lovers. Explore its waterways by boat or wander through ancient villages preserved within the park.

Best Time to Visit: Spring for blooming flowers or autumn for golden reeds.

Chenghuang Pavilion (City God Pavilion)

For panoramic views without the crowds, hike up to this lesser-known pavilion in Wushan Square. It’s a fantastic spot to photograph Hangzhou’s skyline at dusk.

Unique Cultural Experiences

Impression West Lake Show

Directed by Zhang Yimou, this open-air nighttime performance on West Lake combines music, dance, and cutting-edge light effects to tell local legends.

Insider Tip: Book VIP seats for an unobstructed view.

China National Tea Museum

Dive deeper into tea culture at this free museum, where interactive exhibits explain the history and art of Chinese tea. Participate in a traditional tea ceremony for an immersive experience.

Foodie Adventures

Grandma’s Restaurant (Waipo Jia)

A beloved local chain serving authentic Hangzhou dishes. Must-tries include West Lake Vinegar Fish and Shrimp with Longjing Tea.

Night Markets

For a taste of local life, head to Gongshu Night Market or Wushan Night Market, where vendors sell everything from stinky tofu to sweet osmanthus cakes.

Day Trips from Hangzhou

Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

Just two hours away, this stunning lake dotted with islands is ideal for kayaking, cycling, or relaxing in lakeside hot springs.

Wuzhen Water Town

A well-preserved ancient town with charming canals, stone bridges, and Ming-era architecture. Visit during the Wuzhen Theatre Festival for a cultural treat.

Practical Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March–May) for blossoms or autumn (September–November) for pleasant weather.
  • Transport: Rent a bike to explore West Lake, or use the efficient metro system.
  • Etiquette: When visiting temples, dress modestly and avoid loud conversations.

From its timeless landscapes to its vibrant street life, Hangzhou offers endless discoveries. Whether you’re sipping tea in a misty plantation or uncovering hidden alleys, every moment here feels like a page from a classic Chinese poem.
