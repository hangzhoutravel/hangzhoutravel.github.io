Hangzhou’s Most Romantic Restaurants in 2024

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nestled beside the serene West Lake and surrounded by lush tea plantations, Hangzhou has long been a favorite destination for couples seeking a blend of natural beauty and culinary excellence. In 2024, the city’s dining scene continues to evolve, offering intimate settings, breathtaking views, and unforgettable gastronomic experiences. Whether you’re celebrating an anniversary, planning a proposal, or simply indulging in a romantic evening, these restaurants promise to set the perfect mood.

Lakeside Elegance: Dining with a View

1. Xihu Wai Po Jia (西湖外婆家)

Perched along the shores of West Lake, Xihu Wai Po Jia combines traditional Hangzhou flavors with a modern, romantic ambiance. The restaurant’s terrace seating is a hot spot at sunset, where diners can enjoy dishes like Dongpo Pork and West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy while watching the water shimmer under golden hues. The soft glow of lanterns and the distant sound of guzheng music create an enchanting atmosphere.

2. The Moon in the Water (水中月)

Floating on a secluded section of West Lake, this restaurant offers private boat dining experiences. Couples can savor a curated tasting menu featuring seasonal delicacies like Longjing Tea-Infused Shrimp as their boat gently glides past lotus blossoms. The intimate setting, paired with impeccable service, makes it a top choice for marriage proposals.

Hidden Gems: Intimate & Unique

3. Amanfayun’s The Village Restaurant

Tucked within the Amanfayun resort near Lingyin Temple, this restaurant exudes tranquility. Stone pathways lead to private dining nooks surrounded by bamboo groves. The menu highlights Buddhist-inspired vegetarian dishes, such as Braised Bamboo Shoots with Morel Mushrooms, perfect for couples seeking a serene, spiritual dining experience.

4. La Maison de la Forêt (森林小屋)

A French-inspired hideaway in Hangzhou’s tea hills, this boutique restaurant feels like a secret garden. The chef’s tasting menu blends local ingredients with European techniques—think Hangzhou Crab with Saffron Foam. The candlelit courtyard, draped in fairy lights, is pure magic.

Rooftop Romance: Sky-High Dining

5. The Pine at Four Seasons Hangzhou

With panoramic views of West Lake, The Pine offers a sleek, contemporary setting. Their Tea-Smoked Duck is a must-try, and the rooftop bar serves signature cocktails like Dragon Well Martini. Book the “Lovers’ Corner” for a secluded table under the stars.

6. Cloud 9 at Hyatt Regency Hangzhou

Located on the 28th floor, Cloud 9 is all about drama—floor-to-ceiling windows showcase the city skyline. The Wagyu Beef with Black Truffle pairs beautifully with their extensive wine list. Arrive early for golden-hour photos.

Traditional Charm: Time-Honored Romance

7. Lou Wai Lou (楼外楼)

A 160-year-old institution, Lou Wai Lou is where history meets romance. Overlooking West Lake, it’s famed for Beggar’s Chicken (slow-cooked in lotus leaves). The vintage décor and lakeside pavilions transport diners to old-world Hangzhou.

8. Zhi Wei Guan (知味观)

For couples who adore dim sum, this century-old teahouse serves delicate Xiaolongbao and Osmanthus Cake. The private rooms, adorned with calligraphy scrolls, offer quiet intimacy.

Modern Love: Trendy & Chic

9. Bistro été 夏久

A cozy French bistro with a Hangzhou twist, été’s Foie Gras with Longjing Tea Jelly is legendary. The rustic-chic interior, with its exposed brick and flickering candles, feels straight out of Paris.

10. Wild Yeast (野生酵母)

This avant-garde spot focuses on fermentation-inspired dishes, like Fermented Rice with Honey Glazed Eel. The minimalist décor lets the food—and your company—shine.

Tips for a Perfect Romantic Evening

  • Book Ahead: Popular spots like The Moon in the Water require reservations weeks in advance.
  • Sunset Timing: Aim for 5:30–6:30 PM to catch West Lake’s famed “golden hour.”
  • Dress Code: While Hangzhou is relaxed, upscale venues like The Pine appreciate smart casual attire.
  • Post-Dinner Stroll: Walk hand-in-hand along Su Causeway or Nanshan Road’s art galleries.

From floating dinners to sky-high cocktails, Hangzhou’s 2024 romantic dining scene is a feast for the senses. Each restaurant tells a story—yours could be next.
