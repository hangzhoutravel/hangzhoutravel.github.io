Top 10 Things to Do in Hangzhou for an Unforgettable Trip

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, often referred to as "Heaven on Earth," is one of China’s most picturesque cities. With its serene lakes, ancient temples, and lush tea plantations, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Hangzhou has something for everyone. Here are the top 10 things to do in Hangzhou to make your trip unforgettable.

1. Cruise West Lake (Xi Hu)

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without visiting West Lake, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This iconic lake is surrounded by pagodas, gardens, and willow trees, creating a postcard-perfect scene.

Best Ways to Explore West Lake

  • Boat Cruise: Glide across the lake on a traditional wooden boat for stunning views of Leifeng Pagoda and the surrounding hills.
  • Biking: Rent a bike and cycle around the lake’s 15-kilometer perimeter.
  • Walking: Stroll along the Su Causeway or Bai Causeway, especially during sunrise or sunset.

Don’t miss the "West Lake at Night" show, a mesmerizing performance combining music, lights, and water fountains.

2. Visit Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat)

One of China’s most famous Buddhist temples, Lingyin Temple, is nestled at the foot of Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar). The temple complex features intricate carvings, ancient statues, and a peaceful atmosphere.

Highlights

  • Feilai Feng Grottoes: Admire over 470 Buddhist rock carvings dating back to the 10th century.
  • Grand Hall of the Great Sage: Home to a 20-meter-tall statue of Sakyamuni Buddha.
  • Tea Houses Nearby: Sip on Longjing tea after your visit.

3. Hike or Cable Car Up to Leifeng Pagoda

For panoramic views of West Lake, head to Leifeng Pagoda. This five-story tower has a rich history and was rebuilt in 2002 after collapsing in 1924.

Tips for Visiting

  • Hiking: A short but steep hike leads to the pagoda.
  • Elevator: Take the elevator if you prefer an easier ascent.
  • Sunset Views: Time your visit for golden-hour photography.

4. Explore Longjing Tea Plantations

Hangzhou is famous for Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, one of China’s most prized green teas. A visit to the Longjing Village tea fields is a must for tea lovers.

What to Do

  • Tea Tasting: Sample freshly brewed Longjing tea at a local farmhouse.
  • Tea Picking: Try your hand at picking tea leaves during spring (March–April).
  • Learn Tea Culture: Discover the traditional tea-making process.

5. Wander Through Hefang Street

Step back in time on Hefang Street, a bustling pedestrian street lined with traditional shops, snack stalls, and handicraft vendors.

Must-Try Experiences

  • Local Snacks: Try Dongpo Pork, Beggar’s Chicken, and Osmanthus Cake.
  • Silk Shopping: Hangzhou is known for its high-quality silk products.
  • Handicrafts: Watch artisans make fans, scissors, and Chinese medicine.

6. Discover Xixi Wetland Park

Escape the city crowds at Xixi Wetland Park, a tranquil network of rivers, ponds, and marshes. It’s a great spot for nature lovers and photographers.

Activities

  • Boat Ride: Drift through waterways lined with reeds and lotus flowers.
  • Birdwatching: Spot herons and other wetland birds.
  • Ancient Villages: Explore preserved water-town architecture.

7. Admire the Grand Canal

The Hangzhou section of the Grand Canal, the world’s longest artificial waterway, offers a glimpse into China’s imperial past.

What to See

  • Gongchen Bridge: A historic stone bridge with stunning canal views.
  • Museums: Visit the China Grand Canal Museum to learn about its history.
  • Night Cruise: See the canal illuminated at night.

8. Enjoy a Show at Songcheng Park

For entertainment, head to Songcheng Park, a theme park that recreates the Song Dynasty (960–1279 AD).

Highlights

  • "The Romance of the Song Dynasty": A spectacular live performance with acrobatics and dance.
  • Ancient Streets: Walk through replica Song-era buildings.
  • Adventure Rides: Try the "Lost City" virtual reality experience.

9. Visit China National Tea Museum

Tea enthusiasts shouldn’t miss the China National Tea Museum, the only state-level museum dedicated to tea culture.

Exhibits & Experiences

  • Tea History: Learn about China’s 5,000-year tea heritage.
  • Interactive Displays: See how different teas are processed.
  • Tea Ceremony: Participate in a traditional tea-tasting session.

10. Relax at Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

If you have extra time, take a day trip to Qiandao Lake, about 2 hours from Hangzhou. This stunning lake features over 1,000 islands.

Top Activities

  • Island Hopping: Visit Bird Island, Monkey Island, and Lock Island.
  • Kayaking: Paddle through crystal-clear waters.
  • Hot Springs: Unwind at a lakeside resort.

Hangzhou is a city where ancient traditions meet modern charm. Whether you’re sipping tea in a tranquil village or cruising past historic pagodas, every moment here is unforgettable. Plan your trip today and discover why Hangzhou has captivated travelers for centuries!
