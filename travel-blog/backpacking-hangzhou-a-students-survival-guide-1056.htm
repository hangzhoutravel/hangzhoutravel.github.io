Backpacking Hangzhou: A Student’s Survival Guide

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is a dream destination for budget-conscious travelers—especially students. With its serene West Lake, ancient temples, and vibrant street food scene, this city offers an unforgettable experience without breaking the bank. Whether you’re a solo adventurer or traveling with friends, here’s your ultimate guide to exploring Hangzhou like a pro.

Why Hangzhou Should Be on Your Backpacking List

Hangzhou is more than just a pitstop between Shanghai and Beijing. It’s a cultural gem where history meets modernity. The city is famous for:

  • West Lake (Xi Hu): A UNESCO World Heritage Site that’s free to explore.
  • Longjing Tea: The best green tea in China, grown in the nearby hills.
  • Ancient Water Towns: Like Wuzhen and Xitang, easily accessible for day trips.
  • Affordable Street Food: From jianbing (savory crepes) to xiaolongbao (soup dumplings).

Budget-Friendly Accommodation

As a student, every dollar counts. Here are some wallet-friendly stays:

Hostels & Guesthouses

  • Mingtown International Youth Hostel: Near West Lake, with dorm beds starting at $10/night.
  • Loftel Hangzhou: A cozy hostel in a traditional hutong-style building.

Couchsurfing & Homestays

Platforms like Couchsurfing connect you with locals who offer free stays. Alternatively, check out Xiaozhu (similar to Airbnb) for cheap private rooms.

Getting Around Hangzhou on a Budget

Hangzhou’s public transport is efficient and cheap. Here’s how to navigate like a local:

Metro & Buses

  • The metro system covers major attractions (single rides start at $0.50).
  • Buses are even cheaper, but routes can be confusing—use apps like Gaode Maps or Baidu Maps.

Bike Sharing

Hangzhou was one of the first cities in China to adopt bike-sharing. Apps like Hellobike and Meituan Bike offer rides for as low as $0.15 per hour.

Walking

Many attractions, like West Lake and Hefang Street, are best explored on foot.

Must-Visit Attractions (That Won’t Empty Your Wallet)

Free & Cheap Spots

  1. West Lake (Xi Hu): Rent a bike or stroll around the lake—sunset views are priceless.
  2. Hefang Street: A historic pedestrian street with free entry and cheap snacks.
  3. Xixi Wetland Park: Entry is $5, but the natural beauty is worth it.

Student Discounts

  • Lingyin Temple: Show your student ID for half-price tickets (~$3).
  • China National Tea Museum: Free admission, plus free tea tastings!

Eating Like a Local Without Overspending

Hangzhou’s food scene is legendary. Here’s how to eat well on a budget:

Street Food Must-Tries

  • Shengjian Mantou: Pan-fried pork buns (~$1 for 4).
  • Congyou Bing: Scallion pancakes (under $0.50).
  • Ding’s Crispy Duck: A quarter duck costs ~$3.

Cheap Eats Restaurants

  • Grandma’s Home (Waipojia): Local chain with dishes under $5.
  • Xinjiang Fengwei: Uyghur-style lamb skewers for ~$1 each.

Nightlife for Broke Backpackers

You don’t need a fat wallet to enjoy Hangzhou after dark:

Bars & Clubs with Student Vibes

  • Traveller’s Bar: Cheap beers and live music near West Lake.
  • Maya Bar: Happy hour cocktails for $3.

Free Nighttime Activities

  • West Lake Music Fountain Show: Free nightly performances.
  • Nanshan Road Art Street: Perfect for an evening stroll.

Day Trips from Hangzhou

If you have extra time, these nearby spots are worth the trip:

Wuzhen Water Town

  • Cost: ~$15 for entry, but the ancient canals are Instagram gold.
  • How to Get There: Take a bus from Hangzhou East Station (~1.5 hours).

Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

  • Budget Tip: Skip the boat tours and hike instead—free and equally stunning.

Money-Saving Hacks for Backpackers

  1. Use Alipay or WeChat Pay: Many vendors don’t accept cash.
  2. Buy a Hangzhou Public Transport Card: Saves 20% on metro/bus fares.
  3. Visit During Off-Peak Seasons: Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are cheaper and less crowded.

Final Thoughts

Hangzhou is a backpacker’s paradise—affordable, beautiful, and packed with culture. With this guide, you’ll experience the best of the city without stressing over your budget. So pack light, plan smart, and get ready for an adventure you’ll never forget!
