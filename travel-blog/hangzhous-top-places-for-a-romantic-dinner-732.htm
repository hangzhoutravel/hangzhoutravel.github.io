Hangzhou’s Top Places for a Romantic Dinner

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

When it comes to romance, few cities rival Hangzhou’s enchanting blend of natural beauty and culinary excellence. From lakeside fine dining to intimate rooftop settings, the city offers countless spots perfect for a memorable evening with your special someone. Whether you’re celebrating an anniversary, a proposal, or simply indulging in love, here are Hangzhou’s top places for a romantic dinner.

Lakeside Elegance: Dining by West Lake

The Four Seasons Hotel Hangzhou at West Lake

Nestled amid lush gardens and tranquil waterways, The Four Seasons Hotel offers an unparalleled romantic experience. Their signature restaurant, Jin Sha, serves exquisite Cantonese and Hangzhou cuisine in a setting that feels like a private retreat. Reserve a lakeside table at dusk to watch the sunset paint the water in golden hues while savoring dishes like braised Dongpo pork or delicate West Lake fish.

Lou Wai Lou

A legendary establishment overlooking West Lake, Lou Wai Lou combines history with romance. Founded in 1848, this restaurant is famed for its traditional Hangzhou dishes, such as Beggar’s Chicken and West Lake Vinegar Fish. The terrace seating provides uninterrupted views of the lake, making it ideal for couples who appreciate classic charm.

Rooftop Romance: Sky-High Dining

The Pine & Crane at Amanfayun

Perched on a hill near Lingyin Temple, The Pine & Crane at Amanfayun offers a secluded, almost ethereal dining experience. The rooftop terrace overlooks tea fields and ancient forests, creating a serene backdrop for a private dinner. Their seasonal tasting menu, featuring organic local ingredients, is perfect for couples seeking both luxury and intimacy.

Mercato Piccolo

For a more cosmopolitan vibe, head to Mercato Piccolo on the rooftop of the Kerry Centre. This Italian-inspired restaurant serves wood-fired pizzas and handmade pastas alongside panoramic city views. The twinkling lights of Hangzhou at night add a magical touch to any date night.

Hidden Gems: Intimate and Unique

Xixi Wetland’s Slow Boat Dinner

For something truly unforgettable, book a private dinner cruise through Xixi Wetland. Glide along quiet waterways in a traditional wooden boat while enjoying a customized menu of local delicacies. The peaceful surroundings—lush reeds, chirping birds, and the occasional lantern-lit bridge—make this one of Hangzhou’s most romantic hidden experiences.

Vida Garden Restaurant

Tucked away in a restored Qing Dynasty villa, Vida Garden Restaurant exudes old-world romance. The candlelit courtyard, adorned with blooming flowers and trickling fountains, sets the stage for an intimate meal. Their fusion menu blends Hangzhou flavors with Western techniques, offering dishes like tea-smoked duck and truffle-infused dumplings.

Modern Luxury: Chic and Sophisticated

Hyatt Regency Hangzhou’s 28 Hubin Road

If contemporary elegance is your style, 28 Hubin Road delivers with its sleek interiors and inventive cuisine. The restaurant’s floor-to-ceiling windows frame West Lake beautifully, while dishes like foie gras with Longjing tea reduction showcase modern Hangzhou gastronomy.

Bistro 321 Le Bec

Helmed by French chef Nicolas Le Bec, this bistro offers a taste of Paris in Hangzhou. The cozy, dimly lit space is perfect for sharing plates of escargot, duck confit, and decadent desserts. Their wine list, curated with rare French vintages, ensures a toast-worthy evening.

Tea and Tranquility: Romantic Tea Houses

Ziwei Teahouse

For couples who prefer a quieter, more meditative date, Ziwei Teahouse near West Lake is a dreamy choice. Sip premium Longjing tea in a private pavilion surrounded by bamboo groves. Pair your tea with delicate sweets like osmanthus cakes for a truly Zen experience.

Chaxi Teahouse

Located in the historic Hefang Street, Chaxi Teahouse blends tradition with romance. The soft glow of lanterns and the gentle aroma of brewing tea create an atmosphere of timeless love. Opt for their tea-pairing menu, which includes small bites like smoked tofu and honey-glazed nuts.

Final Tips for a Perfect Romantic Evening

  • Timing Matters: Sunset reservations at lakeside or rooftop venues maximize the romantic ambiance.
  • Private Dining: Many high-end restaurants offer private rooms or bespoke menus—don’t hesitate to inquire.
  • Seasonal Specials: Spring and autumn are particularly magical in Hangzhou, with cherry blossoms or golden ginkgo leaves enhancing the scenery.
  • Post-Dinner Strolls: End your evening with a moonlit walk along West Lake or through Nanshan Road’s art galleries.

Hangzhou’s romantic dining scene is as diverse as it is breathtaking. Whether you prefer the timeless allure of West Lake or the modern flair of a rooftop bistro, the city promises an unforgettable evening for you and your loved one.
