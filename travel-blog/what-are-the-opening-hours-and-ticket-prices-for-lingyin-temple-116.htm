What Are the Opening Hours and Ticket Prices for Lingyin Temple?

Home / Travel Blog

Lingyin Temple, also known as the Temple of the Soul’s Retreat, is one of the most famous Buddhist temples in China. Located in Hangzhou, this sacred site draws visitors for its spiritual atmosphere, magnificent architecture, and historical significance. If you're planning a trip to Lingyin Temple, knowing the opening hours and ticket prices will ensure a smooth experience.

The Opening Hours of Lingyin Temple

Lingyin Temple is open to visitors every day of the week, but the hours vary slightly depending on the time of year. Here’s a breakdown of the typical schedule:

  • Spring and Summer (March to October):
    The temple opens at 7:00 AM and closes at 6:00 PM. During these months, the longer daylight hours allow visitors to enjoy more time exploring the temple grounds and surrounding scenic areas.
  • Autumn and Winter (November to February):
    The temple operates from 7:30 AM to 5:30 PM. Shorter days mean less time to explore, so arriving early is recommended.

It’s worth noting that during major Buddhist festivals or public holidays, Lingyin Temple might extend its hours to accommodate larger crowds. Events such as Chinese New Year or Mid-Autumn Festival often attract thousands of pilgrims and tourists, so plan accordingly if you’re visiting during these times.

Ticket Prices for Lingyin Temple

Visiting Lingyin Temple requires the purchase of two separate tickets: one for the Feilai Feng Scenic Area, where the temple is located, and another for the temple itself. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

Feilai Feng Scenic Area Entrance Fee

The Feilai Feng Scenic Area, also known as Flying Peak, is a must-visit before reaching Lingyin Temple. This area features impressive limestone formations and ancient Buddhist carvings, creating a serene prelude to the temple.

  • Price: 45 RMB per person
  • Discounts:
    • Seniors over 70 years old can enter for free with valid ID.
    • Students (with valid student ID) are eligible for discounted tickets, usually priced at 25 RMB.

Lingyin Temple Entrance Fee

Once inside the Feilai Feng Scenic Area, you need a separate ticket to access Lingyin Temple itself.

  • Price: 30 RMB per person
  • Discounts:
    • Similar discounts apply for students and seniors.
    • Children under 1.2 meters in height are generally admitted for free.

Combined Tickets

Some visitors prefer to purchase a combined ticket for convenience. These tickets cover both the Feilai Feng Scenic Area and Lingyin Temple. Prices for combined tickets typically range from 75 RMB to 80 RMB, depending on the time of year and promotions.

Payment Methods

Lingyin Temple accepts multiple payment methods, including cash, credit cards, and digital payments like Alipay or WeChat Pay. Ensure you have the appropriate form of payment ready, especially during peak hours when ticket counters can be busy.

Best Times to Visit

While the temple is open year-round, some times are better suited for a visit than others:

  • Spring (March to May): The blooming flowers and mild weather make this an ideal time to explore both the temple and its surroundings.
  • Autumn (September to November): The vibrant fall foliage adds a picturesque backdrop to your visit, especially around Feilai Feng.
  • Early Morning: Arriving right after the temple opens ensures fewer crowds and a more peaceful experience.
  • Weekdays: To avoid large tourist groups, plan your visit during weekdays rather than weekends or holidays.

Tips for Exploring Lingyin Temple

How to Purchase Tickets

Tickets can be purchased at the entrance or online in advance. Buying tickets online through platforms like Meituan or Fliggy is often faster, especially during busy seasons. Be sure to check if your booking platform offers language support in English if needed.

Guided Tours

For a deeper understanding of Lingyin Temple’s history and significance, consider hiring a guide or joining a tour. Many guides are fluent in English and can provide fascinating insights into the temple’s artifacts, architecture, and Buddhist traditions.

Dress Code

As a sacred site, Lingyin Temple encourages modest attire. Avoid wearing shorts, tank tops, or overly casual clothing out of respect for the temple’s spiritual atmosphere.

Best Spots Within the Temple

  • Hall of the Heavenly Kings: This impressive hall greets visitors with grand statues and intricate carvings.
  • Grand Hall of the Great Sage: Home to the temple’s iconic statue of Buddha, this hall is a highlight for many visitors.
  • Feilai Feng Grottoes: Don’t miss the ancient Buddhist carvings that date back over a thousand years. These grottoes are located near the temple entrance and showcase remarkable craftsmanship.

Accessibility

Lingyin Temple is accessible via public transportation, taxis, or private tours. The temple grounds are generally wheelchair-friendly, with paved paths and ramps in key areas. However, certain parts of Feilai Feng may be challenging to navigate for those with limited mobility.

Nearby Attractions

If you have extra time, consider exploring the surrounding areas:

  • West Lake (Xihu): Just a short drive from Lingyin Temple, West Lake is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its serene beauty and cultural significance.
  • Longjing Tea Plantations: Experience Hangzhou’s famous tea culture by visiting the lush tea fields near Lingyin Temple.
  • Meijiawu Tea Village: This quaint village offers a chance to enjoy traditional tea ceremonies and purchase high-quality Longjing tea.

Lingyin Temple’s combination of spiritual tranquility, historical significance, and natural beauty makes it a must-visit destination in Hangzhou. By understanding its opening hours, ticket prices, and essential tips, you can make the most of your journey to this iconic landmark.
