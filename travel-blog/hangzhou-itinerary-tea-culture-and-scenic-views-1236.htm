Hangzhou Itinerary: Tea Culture and Scenic Views

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is a city where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern charm. Famous for its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. This itinerary focuses on the best of Hangzhou’s tea culture and breathtaking landscapes, ensuring an unforgettable journey.

Day 1: Exploring West Lake and Its Timeless Beauty

Morning: A Leisurely Stroll Around West Lake

Start your day early with a walk along the iconic West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The lake’s shimmering waters, framed by willow trees and distant hills, have inspired poets and artists for centuries.

  • Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao): Begin at this legendary spot, famous for its association with the folktale The Legend of the White Snake.
  • Bai Causeway: Walk along this scenic path, lined with peach blossoms in spring and golden ginkgo leaves in autumn.
  • Leifeng Pagoda: Climb to the top for panoramic views of the lake and the city skyline.

Afternoon: Tea Tasting at Longjing Village

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without experiencing its world-renowned Longjing (Dragon Well) tea. Head to Longjing Village, nestled in the rolling hills just west of West Lake.

  • Tea Plantation Tour: Wander through terraced fields where tea leaves are hand-picked each spring. Learn about the traditional roasting process from local farmers.
  • Tea House Visit: Stop by a family-run tea house for a tasting session. Sip freshly brewed Longjing tea, known for its delicate aroma and smooth, nutty flavor.
  • Local Lunch: Pair your tea with Hangzhou specialties like Dongpo Pork (braised pork belly) or West Lake Vinegar Fish.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

As the sun sets, secure tickets for Impression West Lake, a mesmerizing outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou. Set on the lake itself, this light and sound spectacle blends dance, music, and cutting-edge technology to tell stories of Hangzhou’s history.

Day 2: Temples, Bamboo Forests, and Hidden Gems

Morning: Lingyin Temple and Feilai Feng

Dedicate your morning to Lingyin Temple, one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples.

  • Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar): Explore this limestone grotto adorned with hundreds of ancient Buddhist carvings.
  • Lingyin Temple Complex: Admire the grand halls, giant statues, and tranquil courtyards. Don’t miss the 20-meter-tall Camphorwood Buddha in the Great Hall.

Afternoon: Bamboo Rafting at Xixi Wetland Park

Escape the crowds at Xixi Wetland Park, a sprawling network of waterways, marshes, and traditional villages.

  • Bamboo Raft Ride: Glide through narrow canals shaded by weeping willows and bamboo groves. Keep an eye out for herons and other wildlife.
  • Ancient Water Towns: Visit the preserved Qing Dynasty houses and learn about the area’s fishing and silk-weaving heritage.

Evening: Hefang Street for Souvenirs and Snacks

Wrap up your day at Hefang Street, a lively pedestrian lane brimming with handicrafts, tea shops, and street food.

  • Local Delicacies: Try Beggar’s Chicken (clay-baked chicken), Osmanthus Cake, or Sister Song’s Fish Soup.
  • Souvenir Hunting: Pick up hand-painted fans, silk scarves, or a tin of premium Longjing tea.

Day 3: A Day Trip to Meijiawu Tea Village

Morning: Hands-On Tea Picking Experience

Venture to Meijiawu Tea Village, another hub for Longjing tea production but less touristy than Longjing Village.

  • Tea Picking: Join local farmers in the fields and learn how to select the tenderest tea leaves.
  • Tea Roasting Workshop: Try your hand at roasting leaves in a wok—a skill passed down through generations.

Afternoon: Hiking at Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies

For nature lovers, the Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies trail offers a refreshing hike through tea plantations, forests, and babbling streams.

  • Scenic Walk: Follow the stone paths past waterfalls and moss-covered rocks. The route ends near Longjing Village, perfect for a late lunch.

Evening: Sunset at Baochu Pagoda

End your Hangzhou journey with a climb to Baochu Pagoda, a lesser-known gem with stunning sunset views over West Lake.

Pro Tips for Your Hangzhou Adventure

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) for tea harvest season and autumn (September-November) for pleasant weather.
  • Transport: Use the metro for convenience, but taxis and bikes are great for short trips.
  • Etiquette: When tasting tea, hold the cup with both hands as a sign of respect.

Hangzhou’s blend of natural beauty and cultural depth makes it a traveler’s paradise. Whether you’re sipping tea in a misty plantation or cruising West Lake at dusk, every moment here feels like stepping into a living painting.
