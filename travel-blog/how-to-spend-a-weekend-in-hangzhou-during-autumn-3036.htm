How to Spend a Weekend in Hangzhou During Autumn

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Autumn in Hangzhou is nothing short of magical. As the summer heat gives way to crisp, cool air and the leaves begin their spectacular transformation into shades of gold, crimson, and amber, the city takes on a serene, almost poetic quality. Often referred to as "paradise on earth," Hangzhou is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway during this enchanting season. From the world-famous West Lake to the aromatic tea fields and ancient temples, every corner offers a new discovery. Here’s your ultimate guide to making the most of a weekend in Hangzhou during autumn.

Day 1: Embracing the Essence of West Lake

Morning: A Leisurely Stroll Around West Lake

Start your day early to experience West Lake at its most tranquil. The mist rising from the water in the early morning, combined with the soft autumn light, creates a breathtaking scene. Begin your walk at Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao), famously associated with the legend of the White Snake. As you move along the Bai Causeway, you’ll be surrounded by willow trees and autumn foliage reflecting on the lake’s surface.

Don’t miss the chance to rent a bicycle or take a boat ride to one of the lake’s islands, particularly Three Pools Mirroring the Moon (San Tan Yin Yue). The three small pagodas on the island are iconic, and on autumn evenings, they are illuminated for a mesmerizing effect. However, mornings offer a crowd-free experience perfect for photography and contemplation.

Afternoon: Exploring Lingyin Temple and Feilai Feng

After a morning by the lake, head to Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat), one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples. The temple complex is nestled in a forested area that becomes particularly vibrant in autumn. The golden ginkgo trees and red maple leaves provide a stunning backdrop to the ancient architecture.

Adjacent to the temple is Feilai Feng (Peak Flown From Afar), where you can admire intricate Buddhist carvings etched into limestone cliffs. The hike up is moderate and rewards you with panoramic views of the autumn-colored valley below. This is also a great spot to enjoy a quiet moment away from the city hustle.

Evening: Dining at Hefang Street

As dusk settles, make your way to Hefang Street, a historic pedestrian street that comes alive with traditional architecture, street performances, and local snacks. Autumn evenings here are lively yet cozy. Try Hangzhou’s famous dishes such as Dongpo Pork (braised pork belly), West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy (Xi Hu Cu Yu), and Beggar’s Chicken (Jiao Hua Ji). For dessert, don’t miss the sweet Osmanthus cake, made from the fragrant osmanthus flowers that bloom abundantly in autumn.

Day 2: Tea Culture and Natural Escapes

Morning: Meijiawu Tea Village Experience

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without immersing yourself in its tea culture. Autumn is the season for enjoying Longjing tea (Dragon Well tea), and Meijiawu Tea Village offers an authentic experience. Located in the hills west of West Lake, this village is surrounded by terraced tea plantations that glow in autumn hues.

Join a tea-picking activity (though the main harvest is in spring, autumn offers a quieter experience) or visit a local farmhouse for a tea tasting session. Learn how to properly brew Longjing tea and appreciate its delicate, nutty flavor. The villagers are friendly and often share stories about tea production and local life. This is also a great opportunity to buy freshly packed tea as a souvenir.

Afternoon: Hiking Up to Longjing Village and Nine Creeks

For nature lovers, a hike through the Nine Creeks Meandering Through a Misty Forest (Jiu Xi Yan Shu) is a must-do autumn activity. The trail starts near Longjing Village and winds through bamboo forests, past streams, and under canopies of maple and ginkgo trees. In autumn, the path is carpeted with fallen leaves, and the air is filled with the scent of damp earth and tea plants.

The hike is relatively easy and takes about two hours, ending at the Longjing Village area. Along the way, you’ll encounter small waterfalls and rustic bridges—perfect spots for photos. Once in Longjing Village, you can relax at a tea house overlooking the plantations.

Evening: Enjoying a Sunset at Leifeng Pagoda

End your weekend with a visit to Leifeng Pagoda, which offers the best sunset views over West Lake. The pagoda itself is a modern reconstruction of an ancient structure, but its historical significance (tied to the legend of the White Snake) and elevated position make it a popular spot. As the sun sets, the sky turns shades of orange and pink, casting a warm glow over the lake and surrounding hills.

After descending, take a final leisurely walk along the lake’s southern shore. The evening lights reflecting on the water create a romantic atmosphere, ideal for reflecting on your autumn weekend in Hangzhou.

Practical Tips for an Autumn Weekend in Hangzhou

Best Time to Visit

Autumn in Hangzhou typically spans from late September to November. The peak foliage season is usually mid to late October, when the colors are most vibrant. The weather is cool and comfortable, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), making it ideal for outdoor activities.

What to Pack

Layering is key. Bring a light jacket or sweater for the cooler mornings and evenings, and comfortable walking shoes for exploring. An umbrella might be handy, as autumn can bring occasional drizzles. Don’t forget your camera to capture the stunning landscapes!

Getting Around

Hangzhou has an efficient public transportation system, including a metro and buses. However, for a weekend trip, renting a bicycle is a popular and eco-friendly option. Many areas around West Lake are pedestrian-friendly, and ride-hailing apps like Didi are widely used.

Local Delicacies to Try

Autumn is a season of harvest, and Hangzhou’s culinary scene shines. Besides the classics mentioned earlier, look for seasonal treats like chestnuts, persimmons, and crabs. Street vendors often sell roasted sweet potatoes and sugar-coated hawthorns, which are perfect for snacking while exploring.

Cultural Etiquette

When visiting temples or tea villages, dress modestly and be respectful of local customs. For example, when tasting tea, hold the cup with both hands as a sign of appreciation. Tipping is not customary in China, but it’s always polite to thank your hosts with a smile.

Hangzhou in autumn is a sensory delight—a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re sipping tea in a peaceful village or watching the sunset over West Lake, every moment feels like a page from a classic poem. So pack your bags and get ready to fall in love with this autumn paradise.
