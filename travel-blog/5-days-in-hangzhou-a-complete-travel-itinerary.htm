5 Days in Hangzhou: A Complete Travel Itinerary

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Let's be honest, you don't just visit Hangzhou. You experience it. This is a city that has been polished by poets, revered by emperors, and perfected by time. Often called "Paradise on Earth," Hangzhou is far more than just the home of West Lake. It's a living museum of Chinese culture, a hub of modern innovation, and a culinary destination that will have you dreaming of xiaolongbao for weeks. Forget your typical, rushed tour; this 5-day itinerary is designed to immerse you in the rhythm of the city, balancing iconic sights with hidden gems, ancient traditions with contemporary cool.

Day 1: The Heartbeat of Hangzhou - West Lake & Its Poetic Shores

Your first day is dedicated to the soul of the city: West Lake. Don't just see it; feel it. The lake's beauty isn't in one single landmark but in the ever-changing interplay of water, light, mountain, and willow.

Morning: A Leisurely Lakeside Stroll

Start your day early at Lakeside Park (Hubin). The morning light casting a soft glow on the lake is magical, and you'll see locals practicing Tai Chi, a serene introduction to local life. From here, begin a walk along the Causeways. I recommend starting with the Su Causeway (Sudi), a nearly 3-kilometer path built by the famous poet Su Dongpo. Cross the six iconic arch bridges, each offering a unique, picture-perfect frame of the lake, lotus ponds, and distant hills. The air is fresh, and the atmosphere is tranquil.

Afternoon: An Island Escape & Leifeng Pagoda

Head to the docks and take a boat to Three Pools Mirroring the Moon (Santan Yinyue). This man-made island is the quintessential image of West Lake, famously depicted on the one-yuan bill. Stroll around the islet and marvel at the three small stone pagodas that, during the Mid-Autumn Festival, have candles lit inside them, their light reflecting through holes to create the illusion of multiple moons on the water.

After the island, make your way to the Leifeng Pagoda (Leifeng Ta). While the original collapsed in 1924, the current structure was rebuilt in 2002 atop the old ruins. Take the escalator up (yes, an escalator!) and ascend the pagoda for what is arguably the most breathtaking panoramic view of West Lake. Seeing the lake from above gives you a true sense of its scale and the harmony of its design.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

As dusk falls, prepare for an unforgettable spectacle. "Impression West Lake" is a large-scale open-air performance directed by the renowned Zhang Yimou. Set on the actual water of the lake, with the natural landscape as its backdrop, this show uses light, music, and dance to tell ancient Chinese folktales. It's a mesmerizing fusion of technology and tradition. Book your tickets well in advance, especially during peak season.

Day 2: Spiritual Serenity & A Culinary Pilgrimage

Today we venture beyond the lake to explore one of China's most important spiritual sites and then dive deep into Hangzhou's food scene.

Morning: Lingyin Temple & Feilai Feng Grottoes

Take a short taxi or bus ride to Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul's Retreat). This is one of China's most significant and active Zen Buddhist temples. Before you even enter the main temple hall, you'll walk through the Feilai Feng Grottoes. This area is a labyrinth of limestone rocks and cliffs housing over 470 ancient Buddhist stone carvings, some dating back to the 10th century. The level of detail is astounding. Then, proceed into the temple complex itself. The main hall, the Great Hall of the Great Sage, houses a magnificent 24.8-meter-high camphor-wood statue of Sakyamuni Buddha. The atmosphere is one of profound peace and devotion.

Afternoon: A Feast for the Senses

After a morning of spiritual reflection, it's time for a worldly pleasure: lunch. You cannot leave Hangzhou without trying its most famous dish, Dongpo Pork (Dongporou). Head to a classic restaurant like Lou Wai Lou or Zhi Wei Guan to sample this masterpiece of tender, braised pork belly, named after the same poet who built the Su Causeway. Pair it with another local specialty, West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy (Xihu Cuyu).

Evening: Strolling Through History at Hefang Street

Spend your evening at Hefang Street (Hefang Jie), a restored historic street that wonderfully balances tourist-friendly shops with genuine cultural experiences. Yes, you can buy souvenirs, but you can also watch traditional craftsmen at work, creating everything from scissors and clay figurines to intricate sugar paintings. Visit the Hu Qing Yu Tang Traditional Chinese Medicine Museum, an old pharmacy that feels like a step back in time. For dinner, graze on street food or sit down for a proper meal. Don't miss trying Beggar's Chicken (Jiao Hua Ji), a whole chicken stuffed and baked in a clay shell.

Day 3: Bamboo Forests, Tea Fields, and Water Towns

Escape the city center and discover the lush, green landscapes that surround Hangzhou, which are just as iconic as the lake itself.

Morning: Wuzhen Water Town Day Trip

While a bit of a journey (about an hour by car), a trip to a water town is an essential part of the Jiangnan experience. Wuzhen is one of the most well-preserved and charming. Focus on the Xizha (West Scenic Zone), which is more developed for tourism but incredibly picturesque. Glide along the canals on a wooden boat, passing under ancient stone bridges and alongside Ming and Qing dynasty-era houses. Watch silk weaving demonstrations, explore the Indigo Fabric Workshop, and lose yourself in the maze of cobblestone lanes. The "museum-like" quality of Wuzhen offers a perfectly preserved glimpse into a bygone era.

Afternoon: Sipping Tea at Longjing Village

Return to Hangzhou and head straight into the hills to Longjing Village (Longjing Cun). This is the holy grail for tea lovers, the source of the world-famous Longjing Tea (Dragon Well Tea). As you walk through the stunning, terraced tea fields, you'll understand why this area inspires such reverence. Many local families operate small teahouses. Step into one, sit down, and let them guide you through a proper tea tasting. They will show you how to brew the delicate green leaves and explain the characteristics of the different grades. The simple act of sipping tea while overlooking the fields where it was grown is a profoundly relaxing and authentic Hangzhou experience.

Day 4: Modern Marvels & Creative Sparks

Hangzhou isn't just about its past; it's a dynamic, forward-thinking city. Today is about exploring its contemporary identity.

Morning: The Qianjiang New Town Skyline

Take a trip to the future in Qianjiang New City, Hangzhou's central business district. The contrast with West Lake is staggering. Walk through the Century Park and gaze up at the awe-inspiring modern architecture, including the iconic Hangzhou Grand Theater and the Citizen Center. The centerpiece is the Hangzhou Olympic & International Expo Center, a stunning building that looks like a giant silver globe. This area showcases the economic powerhouse that Hangzhou has become, largely driven by tech giants like Alibaba, which was founded here.

Afternoon: Art & Innovation at the Design Hub

For lunch, explore the restaurants in this modern area before heading to a creative enclave. Visit the China Academy of Art's Xiangshan Campus. The campus itself is an architectural masterpiece, designed by Wang Shu, who won the Pritzker Architecture Prize. The buildings, with their recycled tiles and unique forms, are a work of art.

Alternatively, explore one of the city's many repurposed creative districts, like Xixi National Wetland Park's artistic zones, where old factories have been transformed into galleries, design studios, and chic cafes. It's the perfect place to see the creative energy of modern China.

Evening: A Shopaholic's Paradise

For your final evening, indulge in some retail therapy. Skip the generic malls and head to the IN77 shopping complex at the edge of West Lake. It's a massive, multi-block retail and entertainment destination that is always buzzing with energy. Alternatively, for a more local and eclectic shopping experience, explore the Sichuan Road (Zhongshan Road) area, filled with boutique shops, cool cafes, and lively crowds.

Day 5: Final Impressions & Leisurely Farewells

Your last day should be relaxed, revisiting a favorite spot or discovering one last hidden corner.

Morning: A Bicycle Ride & Hidden Gardens

Rent a public bike (Hangzhou has an excellent bike-share system) and cycle along the Yang Gong Causeway (Yang Gong Di). This causeway is often less crowded than the Su Causeway and offers a wonderfully peaceful and scenic ride through the western part of the lake, passing through tunnels of trees and over quiet bridges.

After your ride, seek out one of the smaller, exquisite gardens around the lake. Guozhuang Garden (Guozhuang) or the Tomb of General Yue Fei are both excellent choices, offering intimate landscapes and rich history away from the main tourist flows.

Afternoon: One Last Bowl of Noodles

For your final meal, make it a simple but satisfying one. Hunt down a bowl of Hangzhou's Pian'er Chuan Noodles, a rich, flavorful noodle soup typically topped with slices of pork and pickled vegetables. It's a beloved local comfort food. After lunch, you might have time for some last-minute souvenir shopping, perhaps for some high-quality Longjing tea or a beautiful silk scarf.

As you make your way to the airport or train station, you'll realize that Hangzhou hasn't just been a destination; it's been a feeling. It's the lingering taste of Dragon Well tea, the reflection of a pagoda in the water, the scent of lotus blossoms on the breeze, and the harmonious blend of a thousand years of history with the vibrant pulse of the future. Five days gives you a beautiful, comprehensive portrait, but you'll leave knowing there are still countless poems left to be read in its landscapes.
