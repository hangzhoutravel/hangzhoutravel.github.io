How to Plan a Short Trip Between Hangzhou and Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog

Hangzhou and Shanghai, two of China's most famous and vibrant cities, are located relatively close to each other. With only a 1-2 hour journey separating them, traveling between these two cities for a short trip is convenient, allowing visitors to experience the best of both worlds. Hangzhou, known for its serene West Lake, and Shanghai, famous for its modern skyline and historical sites, offer a unique contrast that is perfect for a weekend getaway or a brief excursion. Here's a detailed guide to help you plan a smooth and enjoyable short trip between Hangzhou and Shanghai.

Best Time to Travel Between Hangzhou and Shanghai

When planning a short trip, the timing of your visit plays a crucial role in shaping your experience. The best times to travel between Hangzhou and Shanghai are during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) months. These seasons offer pleasant weather, avoiding the intense heat of summer and the cold of winter.

  • Spring: Temperatures range from 12°C to 22°C (54°F to 72°F), perfect for outdoor activities. Both cities look beautiful as flowers bloom and parks come alive with vibrant colors.
  • Autumn: With mild temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F), autumn is ideal for sightseeing and enjoying both cities' natural beauty and cultural landmarks.
  • Summer and Winter: While summer can be hot and humid, winter may be cold and foggy, which could hinder sightseeing. If you do decide to visit during these seasons, be prepared for the weather.

Transportation Options Between Hangzhou and Shanghai

The distance between Hangzhou and Shanghai is about 170 kilometers (around 106 miles), and there are several convenient ways to travel between the two cities. Whether you prefer high-speed trains, buses, or private transportation, there are options available for every budget and schedule.

1. High-Speed Train (Recommended)

One of the most efficient ways to travel between Hangzhou and Shanghai is by taking a high-speed train. The bullet trains (G-series trains) offer fast and comfortable travel, making it an ideal choice for a short trip.

  • Travel Time: The journey takes around 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the specific train.
  • Departure Stations:
    • In Hangzhou: Hangzhou East Station (Hangzhou Dong Zhan) is the primary departure station for high-speed trains to Shanghai.
    • In Shanghai: The most common arrival stations are Shanghai Hongqiao Station and Shanghai Railway Station.
  • Cost: Train tickets usually cost between ¥50 to ¥150, depending on the class of seat and ticket availability. First-class tickets are more expensive than second-class ones, but they offer additional comfort and space.
  • Booking Tickets: You can easily book train tickets online through the China Railway website, various ticket apps, or at the train station.

2. Long-Distance Bus

For a more budget-friendly option, long-distance buses also run frequently between Hangzhou and Shanghai. The journey by bus takes longer, but it is a viable alternative for those on a tighter schedule.

  • Travel Time: The bus ride takes about 2 to 2.5 hours.
  • Cost: Tickets are relatively inexpensive, ranging from ¥30 to ¥80.
  • Departure Stations:
    • In Hangzhou: Buses depart from several locations, such as Hangzhou Passenger Transport Center and Hangzhou East Bus Station.
    • In Shanghai: Arrivals are at major bus stations, including Shanghai South Bus Station or Shanghai General Bus Station.
  • Booking Tickets: Bus tickets can be booked online or purchased at the bus station. It is recommended to arrive early, especially during busy travel seasons.

3. Private Car or Ride-Hailing Services

For maximum comfort and flexibility, hiring a private car or using ride-hailing apps such as DiDi is a great option. This option allows you to travel at your own pace, with door-to-door service and the ability to stop along the way for sightseeing or meals.

  • Travel Time: The journey by car takes approximately 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions.
  • Cost: A private car hire may cost anywhere between ¥300 to ¥600, depending on the vehicle type and service provider. Ride-hailing services might be slightly cheaper.
  • Booking: You can book a private car or ride-hailing service through apps such as DiDi, which provides an English interface.

4. Flights (Not Recommended for Short Trips)

Although Hangzhou and Shanghai have airports with regular flights, flying between the two cities is generally not recommended for a short trip, considering the short distance between them. By the time you factor in airport check-in, security, and travel time to and from the airport, the total travel time may be similar to or longer than the high-speed train.

  • Travel Time: Flight time is around 1 hour, but when you include the airport commute and wait time, it can take 3-4 hours in total.
  • Cost: Flights are usually more expensive than trains, with prices ranging from ¥300 to ¥800 depending on the airline and booking time.

Where to Stay in Hangzhou and Shanghai

Both cities offer a wide range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. If you're staying overnight in either city, it’s important to choose a hotel that is centrally located to maximize your time for sightseeing. Here are some popular areas to stay in:

Hangzhou:

  • West Lake Area: Staying near West Lake is ideal for easy access to Hangzhou’s most famous attraction. Many upscale hotels, boutique inns, and guesthouses are located here.
  • Wulin Square Area: This is a busy commercial area with shopping malls and restaurants. It’s well connected by public transportation, making it convenient for travelers.

Shanghai:

  • The Bund Area: The Bund offers iconic views of the Shanghai skyline, and it is a great place to stay if you want to be close to historical landmarks and scenic walks.
  • People’s Square Area: This central area is close to shopping malls, museums, and theaters, offering easy access to public transportation.
  • Pudong Area: For those looking to stay near the financial district or visit modern attractions like Shanghai Disneyland, the Pudong area is a great option.

Must-See Sights in Hangzhou and Shanghai

While both Hangzhou and Shanghai offer a variety of attractions, their unique cultural and natural characteristics make them stand out. Here are some must-see sights in each city:

Hangzhou:

  1. West Lake: A UNESCO World Heritage site, West Lake is known for its beautiful scenery, historic temples, and traditional pavilions. Enjoy a boat ride on the lake or stroll along its scenic pathways.
  2. Lingyin Temple: One of the most famous Buddhist temples in China, Lingyin Temple offers serene surroundings and beautiful ancient architecture.
  3. Tea Plantations: Hangzhou is famous for its Longjing tea, and you can visit tea plantations to see the tea-making process and enjoy fresh tea.

Shanghai:

  1. The Bund: This famous waterfront area offers stunning views of both colonial-era architecture and the modern skyscrapers of the Pudong area.
  2. Shanghai Tower: The tallest building in China, the Shanghai Tower offers a panoramic view of the city from its observation deck.
  3. Yu Garden: A classical Chinese garden located in the Old City, Yu Garden provides a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Tips for a Successful Short Trip Between Hangzhou and Shanghai

  • Pack Light: Since you’ll be traveling for a short time, try to pack only the essentials to make your journey more comfortable and easy to manage.
  • Use Transportation Apps: Download apps like Baidu Maps or Google Maps to help navigate public transportation and plan your route effectively.
  • Avoid Peak Travel Times: Try to avoid traveling during peak hours or Chinese holidays to minimize delays and crowded trains.
  • Plan for Flexibility: While planning is important, be prepared to adjust your schedule for unforeseen circumstances such as weather or traffic.

With its well-connected transportation options, short travel time, and diverse attractions, a trip between Hangzhou and Shanghai offers a perfect opportunity to explore two of China’s most dynamic cities. Whether you’re looking to relax by West Lake, explore Shanghai’s modern marvels, or experience both cities’ rich culture, this short journey is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
