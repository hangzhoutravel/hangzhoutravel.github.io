10 Best Parks & Gardens to Visit in Hangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, often referred to as "Paradise on Earth," is a city where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Famous for its serene West Lake, the city also boasts an array of stunning parks and gardens that offer tranquility, adventure, and a glimpse into China’s rich horticultural heritage. Whether you’re a nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful escape, these 10 parks and gardens are must-visit destinations in Hangzhou.

1. West Lake (Xi Hu)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site

No visit to Hangzhou is complete without exploring West Lake. This iconic freshwater lake is surrounded by lush gardens, ancient temples, and picturesque bridges. The lake’s beauty changes with the seasons—cherry blossoms in spring, lotus flowers in summer, golden ginkgo leaves in autumn, and misty landscapes in winter.

Must-See Spots

  • Leifeng Pagoda: Offers panoramic views of the lake.
  • Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao): Famous for its romantic legends.
  • Three Pools Mirroring the Moon: A stunning sight, especially during the Mid-Autumn Festival.

2. Hangzhou Botanical Garden

A Botanical Wonderland

Spanning over 200 hectares, the Hangzhou Botanical Garden is one of the largest in China. It features over 3,000 plant species, including rare orchids, towering bamboos, and vibrant azaleas.

Highlights

  • Plum Blossom Valley: A winter spectacle with fragrant blooms.
  • Bamboo Garden: Home to giant bamboos and peaceful walking paths.
  • Lotus Pond: A summer favorite when the flowers are in full bloom.

3. Xixi National Wetland Park

The "Kidneys of Hangzhou"

Xixi is China’s first and only national wetland park, blending water towns, marshes, and lush greenery. Visitors can explore its waterways by boat or stroll along wooden walkways.

Unique Experiences

  • Dragon Boat Rides: Traditional boats glide through narrow canals.
  • Local Handicrafts: Watch artisans make bamboo products and silk.
  • Autumn Reed Snow: A poetic scene when reeds turn golden.

4. Tea Plantations at Longjing Village

The Birthplace of Dragon Well Tea

Nestled in the hills southwest of West Lake, Longjing Village is famed for its premium green tea. The terraced tea fields create a mesmerizing green tapestry.

What to Do

  • Tea Tasting: Sip freshly brewed Longjing tea at a local farmhouse.
  • Hiking Trails: Walk through the plantations for breathtaking views.
  • Learn Tea-Picking: Try your hand at harvesting tea leaves in spring.

5. Prince Bay Park (Tai Zi Wan Gong Yuan)

A Hidden Gem

Located near West Lake, this park is less crowded but equally enchanting. It’s named after a Southern Song Dynasty prince who once lived here.

Features

  • Lotus Ponds: Vibrant blooms in summer.
  • Classic Pavilions: Perfect for quiet contemplation.
  • Cherry Blossom Avenue: A springtime favorite.

6. Hangzhou Safari Park

Fun for Families

This massive safari park combines wildlife encounters with amusement rides. Over 200 animal species roam freely in open habitats.

Top Attractions

  • Safari Bus Tour: Spot lions, tigers, and giraffes up close.
  • Giant Panda Exhibit: Meet Hangzhou’s beloved pandas.
  • Adventure Zone: Roller coasters and animal shows.

7. Guo’s Villa (Guo Zhuang)

A Classical Chinese Garden

Built during the Qing Dynasty, Guo’s Villa is a masterpiece of traditional garden design. Its intricate layouts and water features reflect the harmony of nature and architecture.

Not to Miss

  • Moon Pavilion: Offers stunning lake views.
  • Rockery Maze: A playful labyrinth of stone formations.
  • Lotus Courtyard: A serene spot for tea.

8. Hangzhou Children’s Park

A Playground for Kids

This park is designed for families, with amusement rides, a small zoo, and plenty of open spaces for picnics.

Kid-Friendly Activities

  • Ferris Wheel: Enjoy city views from above.
  • Butterfly House: A colorful exhibit of live butterflies.
  • Paddle Boats: Fun on the water for all ages.

9. White Pagoda Park (Bai Ta Gong Yuan)

A Glimpse of History

This park is home to the White Pagoda, a relic from the Five Dynasties period. It’s a quiet spot with a mix of historical charm and natural beauty.

Why Visit

  • Ancient Architecture: The pagoda is a rare surviving example of its kind.
  • Riverside Walks: Scenic paths along the Qiantang River.
  • Photography: Perfect for capturing Hangzhou’s old-world charm.

10. Hangzhou Flower Nursery

A Floral Paradise

This lesser-known garden is a haven for flower lovers, with seasonal displays of peonies, chrysanthemums, and more.

Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring Tulips: A rainbow of colors in March-April.
  • Summer Roses: Fragrant blooms in June.
  • Autumn Chrysanthemums: Elaborate displays in October.

Hangzhou’s parks and gardens offer endless opportunities to connect with nature, history, and local culture. Whether you’re sipping tea in Longjing or cruising through Xixi’s wetlands, each destination promises a unique and unforgettable experience.
