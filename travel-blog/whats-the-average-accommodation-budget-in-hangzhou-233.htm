What’s the Average Accommodation Budget in Hangzhou?

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou is one of China’s most picturesque and culturally rich cities, making it a top destination for both domestic and international travelers. Whether you're visiting to see the famous West Lake, sipping Longjing tea in a plantation village, or exploring centuries-old temples, one crucial part of your planning involves accommodation. How much should you expect to spend on lodging in Hangzhou? The answer varies widely depending on your preferences, travel style, season, and desired neighborhood.

This guide breaks down the typical accommodation budget in Hangzhou across various categories—from backpacker hostels and budget hotels to luxury five-star resorts and long-term serviced apartments—so you can plan your trip wisely and comfortably.

Budget Travelers: Hostels and Guesthouses ($10–$35 per night)

For solo travelers, students, or those traveling on a tight budget, Hangzhou offers a variety of affordable hostels and guesthouses that don’t compromise on charm or cleanliness. Many of these are located near West Lake, Qinghefang Old Street, or in the more residential areas near Zhejiang University.

Typical Costs

  • Shared dormitory: $10–$20

  • Private room with shared bath: $20–$30

  • Private room with en suite: $30–$35

These accommodations usually offer:

  • Free Wi-Fi

  • Communal kitchens or simple breakfast

  • Lockers

  • Social common areas

Some of the most reputable hostels include:

  • Hangzhou Wushanyi International Youth Hostel

  • The Nook Hostel (close to Xihu)

  • Memory Travel Hostel (near Metro Line 1)

Pros

  • Social atmosphere, ideal for meeting fellow travelers

  • Often managed by friendly bilingual staff

  • Great location for sightseeing and transport

Cons

  • Limited privacy in shared rooms

  • Can get noisy during peak travel seasons

  • Amenities are basic

Mid-Range Comfort: 3-Star Hotels and Boutique Lodging ($40–$80 per night)

This price range gives you access to a more comfortable, often locally styled experience with better room amenities and customer service. Many mid-range hotels in Hangzhou are operated by national chains or as independent boutique properties.

Typical Inclusions

  • Private bathroom

  • Air conditioning

  • Daily housekeeping

  • TV and minibar

  • Breakfast buffet (sometimes included)

Neighborhoods like Wulin Square, Binjiang, and Huanglong offer many of these options, with proximity to metro stations and shopping centers.

Popular choices in this category include:

  • SSAW Boutique Hotel Hangzhou

  • Elan Hotel (near Hangzhou East Station)

  • Holiday Inn Express Hangzhou East

These places are suitable for business travelers, couples, or families looking for a balance between affordability and comfort.

Upper Mid-Range: 4-Star Hotels and Upscale Boutique Inns ($90–$150 per night)

Hangzhou shines in this category, with many architecturally beautiful boutique hotels built in the traditional Jiangnan style. Think classic rooftops, bamboo courtyards, and serene aesthetics—perfect for travelers who want an immersive cultural experience without splurging completely.

Popular areas like Hubin (near West Lake), Yuhuangshan, and the tea fields in Longjing village offer memorable stays in this price range.

Expect amenities such as:

  • Spacious rooms with views of nature or the lake

  • On-site dining with regional cuisine

  • English-speaking concierge

  • Fitness centers and spa services

  • Airport shuttle service (sometimes included)

Top picks include:

  • Tea Boutique Hotel

  • Azure Qiantang by Radisson

  • Pins De La Brume Hotel in Lingyin scenic area

This tier is often favored by couples on honeymoon, seasoned travelers, or those seeking a touch of luxury without exceeding their budget.

Luxury and 5-Star Hotels ($160–$400+ per night)

For those seeking luxury, Hangzhou is home to world-class hotels with panoramic views, top-tier service, and designer architecture. This category includes both Chinese and international chains and is mainly concentrated in the West Lake area, Qianjiang New Town, and Binjiang.

Flagship properties include:

  • Four Seasons Hotel Hangzhou at West Lake

  • Park Hyatt Hangzhou

  • InterContinental Hangzhou (shaped like a golden globe)

  • Amanfayun (located in a reimagined village with private villas)

What You Get

  • Spa and wellness centers

  • Indoor swimming pools

  • Michelin-star restaurants or equivalent

  • Business and conference services

  • Lakeside or skyline views

  • 24-hour butler or concierge service

These hotels are often chosen by business executives, luxury honeymooners, or high-net-worth individuals wanting tranquility and pampering.

Family Accommodation Options ($50–$150 per night)

Traveling with children? Hangzhou is a family-friendly city with several kid-accommodating hotels and short-term rental apartments. Families often opt for:

  • Serviced apartments like Ascott Raffles City Hangzhou

  • Family suites in mid-range hotels

  • Short-term Airbnb-style rentals (though platforms may require VPN or workarounds to access)

Amenities to look for:

  • Cribs and extra beds

  • Kitchenette

  • Washing machines

  • Kid-friendly breakfast options

  • Close proximity to parks and attractions

A typical family of four can expect to spend:

  • Budget apartment: $50–$70

  • Hotel suite: $90–$150

  • Luxury family resort: $180+

Long-Term Stay: Serviced Apartments ($600–$2,000 per month)

For digital nomads, expats, or long-term tourists, Hangzhou’s serviced apartments offer both flexibility and comfort. Locations like Binjiang, Qianjiang Century City, and areas near Hangzhou International School are ideal.

Monthly costs vary:

  • Studio: $600–$900

  • One-bedroom: $1,000–$1,500

  • Two-bedroom luxury units: $1,800–$2,000

Popular brands include:

  • Fraser Suites

  • Oakwood Residence

  • Citadines Intime City Hangzhou

These rentals usually include:

  • Full kitchen

  • Weekly housekeeping

  • Gym access

  • 24-hour reception

  • Utility bills (sometimes)

Many long-term visitors appreciate the ability to cook, work, and relax like a local, especially when staying for several weeks or more.

Seasonal Price Variations

Accommodation pricing in Hangzhou is influenced by the time of year. Expect higher rates during:

  • National holidays (especially Golden Week in October and Spring Festival in January/February)

  • Cherry blossom season in March

  • Summer months (June–August), particularly for West Lake area stays

  • Mid-autumn festival (September)

Off-season discounts are available in late fall and winter, where hotel rates may drop by 20–40%.

Booking Platforms and Tips

To find the best deals, compare platforms like:

  • Booking.com (most foreigner-friendly)

  • Agoda

  • Ctrip (can be navigated in English)

  • Airbnb (for apartments, though use may require VPN)

Tips for booking:

  • Book early if traveling during major Chinese holidays

  • Double-check the location—some listings mention "West Lake area" but are actually a 20-minute drive away

  • Ask about elevator access in older buildings

  • Contact hotels directly for promotions not listed on platforms

  • Verify whether breakfast is included in the rate

Hidden Costs to Consider

When budgeting for accommodation, factor in:

  • Security deposit: Especially for apartments, may range from $100–$300

  • Taxes and service charges: Sometimes an additional 10–15%

  • Laundry fees: Usually charged by piece or by weight

  • Transportation: Staying far from metro stations could increase taxi or Didi costs

  • Visa extension support: Some hotels provide visa assistance for long-term guests

Hangzhou’s wide variety of accommodation ensures that everyone—from frugal backpackers to luxury aficionados—can find a place to rest, recharge, and experience the city in their own style. With proper research and timing, it’s possible to enjoy this scenic, historic city without blowing your budget.
