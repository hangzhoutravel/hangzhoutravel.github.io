Hangzhou’s Top Scenic Drives & Road Trips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, a city where ancient charm meets modern elegance, is a paradise for road trippers. With its lush landscapes, serene lakes, and winding mountain roads, it offers some of the most breathtaking scenic drives in China. Whether you're cruising along the West Lake, exploring tea plantations, or venturing into the bamboo forests, every route promises an unforgettable adventure. Here’s your ultimate guide to Hangzhou’s best road trips and scenic drives.

The West Lake Loop: A Classic Drive

Why It’s a Must-Drive

The West Lake (Xi Hu) is the heart of Hangzhou, and driving around its 15-kilometer loop is a mesmerizing experience. The route takes you past willow-lined boulevards, historic pagodas, and tranquil gardens. Early mornings or late afternoons are ideal for avoiding crowds and catching the golden glow over the water.

Highlights Along the Way

  • Leifeng Pagoda: Stop here for panoramic views of the lake.
  • Su Causeway: A picturesque stretch perfect for a quick stroll or photo op.
  • Lingyin Temple Area: Detour slightly to explore one of China’s most famous Buddhist temples.

Pro tip: Rent a bike or electric scooter for parts of the loop to fully immerse yourself in the scenery.

Longjing Tea Plantations: A Green Escape

The Route Through Tea Country

Just southwest of West Lake, the rolling hills of Longjing (Dragon Well) are home to some of China’s finest tea plantations. The narrow, winding roads here are flanked by emerald-green tea terraces, offering a serene drive through Hangzhou’s agricultural heritage.

Best Stops

  • Meijiawu Tea Village: Sample freshly brewed Longjing tea and learn about tea-picking traditions.
  • Eighteen Imperial Tea Bushes: A historic site where emperors once sourced their tea.
  • Cloudy Bamboo Path (Yunqi Zhujing): A hidden gem with a bamboo-lined trail perfect for a short hike.

Timing is key—visit in spring (March-April) during the tea harvest for the liveliest atmosphere.

Thousand-Island Lake (Qiandao Hu): A Lakeside Adventure

The Scenic Drive from Hangzhou

About 150 kilometers southwest of Hangzhou, Qiandao Hu is a stunning man-made lake with over a thousand islands. The 2.5-hour drive is part of the fun, with highways cutting through lush valleys and small villages.

What to Explore

  • Island Hopping: Take a ferry to islands like Bird Island or Lock Island.
  • Xiandu Scenic Area: A detour worth taking for its dramatic karst landscapes.
  • Lakeside Resorts: Spend a night in a waterfront villa for a tranquil retreat.

For photography enthusiasts, sunrise over the mist-covered lake is pure magic.

Tianmushan Road: A Thrilling Mountain Drive

The Ultimate Hairpin Experience

Tianmushan (Heavenly Eyes Mountain), located 80 kilometers west of Hangzhou, boasts one of the most exhilarating drives in the region. The road snakes up the mountain with sharp turns and steep cliffs, offering adrenaline-pumping views.

Key Attractions

  • Tianmushan National Park: Hike among ancient trees and waterfalls.
  • Grand Canyon of Zhejiang: A lesser-known but stunning natural wonder.
  • Buddhist Temples: Discover secluded monasteries tucked into the hills.

Drive cautiously—this route is as challenging as it is beautiful.

Xixi Wetland: A Peaceful Urban Retreat

A Short but Scenic Escape

For those short on time, Xixi Wetland offers a quick yet rewarding drive just 5 kilometers from downtown Hangzhou. The area combines waterways, traditional architecture, and lush greenery, making it a unique urban oasis.

Don’t Miss

  • Boat Rides: Glide through the wetlands on a traditional wooden boat.
  • Ancient Villages: Explore restored Ming and Qing dynasty houses.
  • Local Snacks: Try Hangzhou specialties like congyoubing (scallion pancakes) from street vendors.

Visit during weekdays to avoid weekend crowds.

Hangzhou Bay Bridge: An Engineering Marvel

Coastal Views on China’s Longest Sea Bridge

Stretching 36 kilometers across Hangzhou Bay, this bridge is a feat of modern engineering. The drive offers sweeping ocean views and a chance to witness the meeting point of the Qiantang River and the East China Sea.

Nearby Detours

  • Nanbeihu Scenic Area: A quiet lakeside spot for a picnic.
  • Cixi: A small city known for its ceramics and seafood.

Time your drive to coincide with the Qiantang River Tidal Bore for a spectacular natural show.

Tips for the Perfect Hangzhou Road Trip

  • Rent a Comfortable Vehicle: Opt for a car with good suspension for mountain roads.
  • Pack Light Snacks: Hangzhou’s countryside has limited rest stops.
  • Check Weather Conditions: Mountain roads can be slippery after rain.
  • Use Local Apps: Amap or Baidu Maps are more reliable than Google Maps in China.

Hangzhou’s diverse landscapes ensure every road trip is a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, these routes promise memories that last a lifetime.
