How to Travel from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou East Station, one of the busiest transportation hubs in China, serves as a gateway for many visitors arriving in the city. West Lake, the iconic heart of Hangzhou, is a must-see destination known for its serene beauty and rich cultural heritage. For travelers, getting from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake is a straightforward journey with several transportation options. Here’s an in-depth guide to help you navigate your way efficiently and comfortably.

Taking the Metro from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake

The Hangzhou Metro is a convenient and cost-effective way to travel between Hangzhou East Station and West Lake.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Board Line 1 at Hangzhou East Station:
    Start your journey by taking Metro Line 1, which is accessible from the station. The metro signs are clearly marked in English, making it easy for non-Chinese speakers to navigate.

  2. Transfer to Line 2 or Line 7 (Optional):
    Depending on your final destination around West Lake, you may need to transfer to another line. For instance:

    • If heading toward the northern part of West Lake, transfer to Line 2 at Fengqi Road Station.
    • For other areas, you may connect to Line 7.

  3. Get off at Fengqi Road or Longxiangqiao Station:
    These stations are closest to West Lake's eastern shore. From here, it’s a short walk to reach the lake area.

  4. Estimated Travel Time and Costs:
    The metro journey typically takes around 30-40 minutes, and the fare is approximately ¥4-6, depending on the exact route.

Tips for Metro Travelers:

  • Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:30 PM) for a more comfortable experience.
  • Use the Hangzhou Metro app or maps available at the station for route planning.

Taking a Taxi from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake

For a more direct and comfortable option, taking a taxi is ideal. Taxis are readily available outside Hangzhou East Station.

Key Details:

  1. Estimated Travel Time:
    A taxi ride typically takes 20-30 minutes, depending on traffic conditions.

  2. Cost:
    The fare ranges between ¥40-60. Keep in mind that fares may increase during peak hours or if there are traffic delays.

  3. Tips for Taking a Taxi:

    • Have your destination written in pinyin or English. For example, you can write “Xi Hu” for West Lake.
    • Ensure the driver uses the meter to avoid overcharging.

  4. Ride-Hailing Apps:
    If you prefer a cashless option, apps like DiDi Chuxing allow you to book a ride easily. The app supports English interfaces and international credit cards.

Taking a Bus from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake

Public buses are an economical option, though they may take longer than the metro or taxis.

Popular Bus Routes:

  • Bus 28:
    This route connects Hangzhou East Station to various stops around West Lake. It’s a great option for travelers heading toward the southern or western parts of the lake.

  • Other Local Buses:
    Many buses operate between Hangzhou East Station and the city center, where West Lake is located. Look for buses heading toward destinations like Nanshan Road, Beishan Road, or Pinghai Road.

Details to Consider:

  1. Cost:
    Bus fares are typically ¥2-3, making them the most budget-friendly option.

  2. Travel Time:
    Depending on traffic and the specific route, buses can take 45 minutes to over an hour.

  3. Tips for Taking the Bus:

    • Use a transportation app or check station maps for accurate route numbers.
    • Be prepared with small change, as buses may not provide change for cash payments.

Renting a Bicycle to Explore West Lake

Once you reach West Lake, renting a bicycle is a fantastic way to explore the scenic paths around the lake. Hangzhou is known for its extensive bike-sharing system and cycle-friendly environment.

How to Rent a Bike:

  1. Public Bike-Sharing Services:
    Hangzhou Public Bicycle System offers bikes at numerous stations near West Lake. You’ll need to register and pay a small deposit.

  2. Private Bike Rentals:
    Many private vendors offer bike rentals at reasonable rates, typically around ¥10-20 per hour.

  3. Mobike or HelloBike Apps:
    Download bike-sharing apps to locate and unlock bikes near you. These apps are convenient and widely used by locals and tourists alike.

Cycling Tips:

  • Stick to designated bike lanes and paths around the lake.
  • Early mornings and late afternoons offer the best weather for cycling.

Walking from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake

For adventurous travelers or those who enjoy walking, it’s possible to reach West Lake on foot, though it’s a long journey. The distance is approximately 8-10 kilometers, taking around 2-3 hours.

Walking Route:

  1. Start from Hangzhou East Station:
    Head west toward the city center, following signs for major roads leading to West Lake.

  2. Pass Through Key Streets:
    Streets like Tianmushan Road and Wulin Road are pedestrian-friendly and filled with interesting sights, cafes, and shops along the way.

  3. Arrive at West Lake:
    You’ll enter the eastern shore of West Lake, with iconic landmarks like Broken Bridge and Bai Causeway nearby.

Walking Tips:

  • Wear comfortable shoes and bring water.
  • Use a navigation app to stay on course.

Choosing the Best Option

The best way to travel from Hangzhou East Station to West Lake depends on your priorities:

  • For speed and convenience: Take a taxi or use ride-hailing apps.
  • For cost-effectiveness: Use the metro or buses.
  • For an immersive experience: Walk or rent a bike upon arrival.

No matter which mode of transportation you choose, the journey to West Lake is the beginning of an unforgettable Hangzhou adventure. The lake’s stunning scenery, historical landmarks, and cultural richness make it a destination worth exploring in detail.
