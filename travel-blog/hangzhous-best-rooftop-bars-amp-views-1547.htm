Hangzhou’s Best Rooftop Bars & Views

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, a city where ancient charm meets modern luxury, is famous for its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and vibrant nightlife. But for those who want to take their experience to new heights—literally—the city’s rooftop bars offer the perfect blend of craft cocktails, gourmet bites, and breathtaking views. Whether you’re sipping a martini under the stars or enjoying a sunset with a local Longjing tea-infused cocktail, these elevated venues are a must-visit.

The Ultimate Rooftop Experience in Hangzhou

1. Cloud 9 at The Hangzhou Marriott Hotel Qianjiang

Perched on the 49th floor, Cloud 9 is one of the highest rooftop bars in the city, offering panoramic views of the Qiantang River and the glittering skyline. The sleek, modern design pairs perfectly with their signature drinks like the Qiantang Breeze—a refreshing mix of gin, elderflower, and citrus.

Why Visit?
- Unmatched 360-degree city views
- Live DJ sets on weekends
- A curated selection of Asian-inspired tapas

2. Xanadu Rooftop Bar at The Wyndham Grand Plaza Royale

Nestled near West Lake, Xanadu combines luxury with an intimate vibe. The bar’s lush greenery and cozy cabanas make it a favorite for romantic evenings. Don’t miss their West Lake Sunset, a cocktail blending Hangzhou’s famous Osmanthus wine with sparkling prosecco.

Highlights:
- Stunning West Lake vistas
- Exclusive VIP lounges
- Seasonal cocktail menus

3. The Moon & The Desert People at The Hyatt Regency

This bohemian-chic rooftop spot is all about relaxed elegance. With floor cushions, lantern-lit tables, and a menu featuring Middle Eastern-inspired small plates, it’s a unique escape from the city bustle. Try their Silk Road Spritz, a twist on the classic Aperol spritz with saffron-infused vodka.

Don’t Miss:
- Themed nights (think Moroccan lounges or jazz evenings)
- An extensive shisha menu
- Instagram-worthy decor

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Rooftops

4. The Secret Garden at The Azure Qiantang

Tucked away in the Binjiang district, this rooftop feels like a private oasis. The bar specializes in herbal-infused cocktails, like the Dragon Well Mule, made with local Longjing tea and ginger beer.

Why It’s Special:
- Quiet, uncrowded atmosphere
- A focus on locally sourced ingredients
- Stunning sunset views over the river

5. The Loft at The Four Seasons Hangzhou

While not a traditional rooftop, The Loft’s terrace offers unparalleled views of West Lake’s pagodas and willow-lined shores. Their Lotus Martini, garnished with an edible flower, is as photogenic as the scenery.

Perks:
- Five-star service
- A menu pairing cocktails with Hangzhou delicacies (think drunken crab canapés)
- Private tastings with master mixologists

Rooftop Bars with a Twist

6. The Brew at The Aloft Hangzhou Wetland Park

For craft beer lovers, this industrial-chic rooftop microbrewery is a dream. Their Wetland IPA is brewed on-site, and the laid-back vibe attracts a young, trendy crowd.

Standout Features:
- Brewmaster-led tours
- Weekend BBQ nights
- Games like giant Jenga and foosball

7. The Green Mansion at The Park Hyatt Hangzhou

This eco-conscious rooftop bar is draped in vertical gardens and serves zero-waste cocktails. The Hangzhou Sour, made with upcycled fruit peels and local baijiu, is a must-try.

Eco-Perks:
- Solar-powered lighting
- Compostable straws and utensils
- A rooftop herb garden used in drinks

Pro Tips for Rooftop Hopping in Hangzhou

  • Timing is Key: Arrive by 5 PM to snag the best seats for sunset.
  • Dress Code: Most upscale bars enforce a smart-casual dress code (no flip-flops!).
  • Reservations: Book ahead for weekends, especially at venues like Cloud 9 or Xanadu.
  • Local Flavors: Opt for drinks infused with Longjing tea, Osmanthus, or bamboo—it’s a true taste of Hangzhou.

From sky-high luxury to hidden garden retreats, Hangzhou’s rooftop bars are as diverse as they are dazzling. Whether you’re a cocktail connoisseur or just chasing the perfect sunset selfie, these spots promise an unforgettable night out in one of China’s most picturesque cities.
