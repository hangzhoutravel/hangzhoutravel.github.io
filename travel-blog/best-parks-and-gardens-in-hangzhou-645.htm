Best Parks and Gardens in Hangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, often referred to as "Heaven on Earth," is a city where nature and urban life blend seamlessly. Famous for its serene West Lake, Hangzhou also boasts some of the most beautiful parks and gardens in China. Whether you’re a nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful retreat, these green spaces offer something for everyone.

West Lake Scenic Area: The Crown Jewel

No visit to Hangzhou is complete without exploring the West Lake Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This iconic destination is more than just a lake—it’s a sprawling landscape of gardens, pagodas, and historic sites.

Must-Visit Spots Around West Lake

  1. Su Causeway (Sudi)
    Named after the famous poet Su Shi, this tree-lined pathway stretches nearly 3 kilometers and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride. In spring, the peach blossoms and willow trees create a dreamy atmosphere.

  2. Leifeng Pagoda
    Overlooking the lake, this pagoda offers panoramic views, especially at sunset. The surrounding gardens are lush, making it a great spot for photos.

  3. Flower Harbor Park (Huagang Guanyu)
    Known for its vibrant peony garden and koi-filled ponds, this park is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Don’t miss the "Viewing Fish at Flower Harbor" experience.

Hangzhou Botanical Garden: A Floral Wonderland

For plant enthusiasts, the Hangzhou Botanical Garden is a must-see. Spanning over 200 hectares, it’s one of the largest botanical gardens in China.

Highlights of the Garden

  • Cherry Blossom Avenue (March-April): A breathtaking tunnel of pink blooms.
  • Bamboo Garden: Home to rare species like the Phyllostachys edulis (Moso Bamboo).
  • Lotus Ponds: In summer, the ponds burst with colorful lotus flowers.

Pro tip: Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy the tranquil ambiance.

Xixi National Wetland Park: A Hidden Gem

If you prefer off-the-beaten-path experiences, Xixi Wetland Park is Hangzhou’s best-kept secret. This ecological reserve features waterways, traditional villages, and lush greenery.

What to Do in Xixi Wetland

  • Take a Boat Ride: Glide through narrow canals and under ancient bridges.
  • Explore Shen Zhuang Xu Village: A restored water town with charming tea houses.
  • Birdwatching: Over 100 bird species inhabit the wetlands, making it a paradise for bird lovers.

Tea Plantations: Longjing Village and Beyond

Hangzhou is synonymous with Longjing tea (Dragon Well tea), and visiting a tea plantation is a unique way to connect with local culture.

Best Tea Gardens to Visit

  1. Meijiawu Tea Village
    Less touristy than Longjing Village, Meijiawu offers authentic tea-picking experiences. You can even join a tea ceremony.

  2. China National Tea Museum
    Located near Longjing Village, this museum provides insights into tea history and cultivation. The surrounding gardens are equally impressive.

Hangzhou Safari Park: Fun for Families

For a mix of nature and adventure, Hangzhou Safari Park is a fantastic choice. It’s one of the largest safari parks in Asia, home to over 200 animal species.

Top Attractions

  • Safari Bus Tour: Spot lions, tigers, and giraffes in open habitats.
  • Giant Panda Exhibit: Meet Hangzhou’s beloved pandas.
  • Walking Zones: Explore themed areas like the African Savannah and Tropical Rainforest.

Tips for Visiting Hangzhou’s Parks and Gardens

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) for blossoms; autumn (September-November) for pleasant weather.
  • Transport: Rent a bike to explore West Lake or use the metro for farther locations.
  • Etiquette: Respect nature—avoid littering and stick to designated paths.

Hangzhou’s parks and gardens are more than just scenic spots—they’re gateways to the city’s soul. Whether you’re sipping tea in a misty plantation or wandering through a wetland, each green space tells a story of harmony between humans and nature.
