Best Summer Day Trips from Hangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

The summer sun hangs heavy over the West Lake, its famous beauty shimmering in the heat. While the willow trees offer some respite, there's a whole world of incredible landscapes, ancient towns, and cool retreats waiting just a short journey from Hangzhou. When the city humidity becomes too much, it's time to embark on an adventure. Forget long, complicated vacations; the magic of Eastern China is that its greatest treasures are often just a day trip away. We've curated the ultimate list of getaways to help you beat the heat and discover the profound beauty surrounding this legendary city. Pack your sunscreen, a reusable water bottle, and a sense of wonder—it's time to explore.

The Water & Mountain Escape: Qiandao Lake

Imagine a vast, blue-green expanse dotted with 1,078 emerald islands. This is Qiandao Lake, or "Thousand Island Lake," a man-made masterpiece that feels entirely natural. Created after the construction of the Xin'an River Dam, the lake submerged ancient valleys and hills, leaving only the peaks visible and creating a surreal, breathtaking landscape. It’s the perfect antidote to a summer scorcher.

Why It's a Perfect Summer Trip

The temperature here is consistently several degrees cooler than in downtown Hangzhou. The sheer volume of water acts as a natural air conditioner. The primary activities—boating, swimming, and lakeside strolls—are all centered around staying cool. The air is famously fresh, making it a literal breath of fresh air.

Must-Do Activities

Your day revolves around the water. Start with a scenic cruise. Most tours will take you to a selection of themed islands. Meifeng Island offers a panoramic viewing platform that gives you a staggering 360-degree view of the islands scattered to the horizon. For a bit of quirky fun, Lock Island is covered with thousands of padlocks left by visiting couples, symbolizing their everlasting love.

If you're feeling more active, skip the big cruise and rent a kayak or a paddleboard. Gliding through the calm, turquoise waters, weaving between the small, forested islands, is an incredibly peaceful and immersive experience. For the truly adventurous, Qiandao Lake is home to some of the best freshwater diving in China. The underwater visibility is remarkable, and you can explore the submerged ancient cities, including the famed Lion City (Shi Cheng), a time capsule from the Ming and Qing dynasties. This requires planning and a certified guide, but it's a truly unique world-class adventure.

Getting There & Pro Tip

The most efficient way to get to Qiandao Lake is by high-speed train from Hangzhou East Station. The journey takes about an hour. From the Qiandao Lake station, you can take a short taxi or bus ride to the main scenic area. Pro Tip: Book your cruise tickets online in advance during peak summer weekends to avoid long queues. Pack a swimsuit and a quick-dry towel—you won't regret it.

The Ancient Canal Town: Wuzhen (Xizha)

Step back in time and experience the romantic, water-bound charm of a classic Jiangnan town. Wuzhen is arguably the most famous of these canal towns, and for good reason. It's meticulously preserved, offering a vivid picture of life along the ancient Grand Canal. While it can be busy, a summer visit allows you to enjoy its beauty late into the balmy evenings.

Why It's a Perfect Summer Trip

Summer is the season of the Wuzhen Drama Festival, a world-class event that transforms the entire town into a stage. Even outside the festival, the town has a magical atmosphere after dark. The heat of the day gives way to a pleasant coolness, and the canals are lit by traditional lanterns, creating a scene straight out of a classical painting. It’s a cultural and aesthetic cooling of the senses.

Must-Do Activities

Resist the urge to rush. The best way to experience Wuzhen is to wander aimlessly. Get lost in the labyrinth of ancient flagstone alleys, cross the time-worn stone bridges, and watch the world go by from the shaded corridors that line the canals. Visit the traditional workshops where you can see indigo blue fabric printing—a local specialty—and perhaps even try your hand at making a small piece.

As dusk settles, the town's true magic unfolds. Take a gondola ride along the canals. As your boatman poles you through the quiet waterways, the reflections of the red lanterns in the water are simply mesmerizing. This is the coolest and most romantic part of the day. Afterwards, find a waterside restaurant and sample local delicacies like braised pork belly (hong shao rou) and freshwater shrimp.

Getting There & Pro Tip

Buses from Hangzhou's main bus stations (Jiubao is a major one) run frequently to Wuzhen and take about 1.5 hours. You can also combine a trip with a ride-sharing service for more flexibility. Pro Tip: Focus your visit on the Xizha (West Scenic Zone). It's more restored and less crowded with tourist groups than the Dongzha (East) section, especially in the evening. Stay until after 8 PM to see the town at its most beautiful and peaceful.

The Bamboo Forest & Martial Arts Pilgrimage: Moganshan

For a completely different kind of cool, head for the hills. Moganshan is a lush bamboo-covered mountain retreat that has been a haven for foreigners and wealthy Shanghainese since the early 20th century. It’s a place of profound tranquility, stunning vistas, and a deep connection to nature.

Why It's a Perfect Summer Trip

The elevation provides a natural escape from the sweltering lowland heat. It's not uncommon for temperatures to be 10°F (5-6°C) cooler. The dense bamboo forest creates a deeply shaded, cool environment perfect for hiking. The mountain is also famously quiet, with only the sound of the wind rustling through the bamboo—a phenomenon known as the "Moganshan Whisper."

Must-Do Activities

Hiking is the main event. There are numerous trails winding through the breathtaking bamboo forests. One of the most popular hikes is to the Jianchi Pond, a refreshingly cool spot to rest. The summit offers fantastic views of the surrounding countryside. For a touch of history, seek out the old European-style villas built by missionaries and traders a century ago; many have been converted into boutique hotels and cafes.

Moganshan is also intrinsically linked to the world of wuxia (martial arts heroes). It was a key filming location for the movie Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon. As you walk through the towering bamboo groves, you can almost imagine Li Mubai and Yu Xiulian gracefully dueling above you in the treetops. It’s a must for any film fan.

Getting There & Pro Tip

The easiest way is to take a high-speed train from Hangzhou to Deqing Station (about 20 minutes), followed by a 30-minute taxi ride up the mountain. Pro Tip: Wear sturdy, comfortable shoes for hiking. The paths can be slippery. Also, book a table for lunch or tea at one of the mountain-top resorts with a view; the combination of cool breeze and panoramic scenery is unforgettable.

The Tea & Temple Serenity: Hangzhou's Own Backyard - Meijiawu

You don't always have to travel far to find a perfect summer day. Nestled in the hills just southwest of West Lake lies Meijiawu, the heart of Hangzhou's legendary Longjing tea country. This is a trip for the senses, focusing on taste, smell, and serene beauty.

Why It's a Perfect Summer Trip

It's incredibly close and accessible, making it a perfect half-day or lazy full-day excursion. The tea plantations are terraced on hillsides, catching breezes that skip over the city. Sitting in a farmer's teahouse, sipping the freshest tea imaginable while overlooking the vibrant green fields, is a profoundly cooling and meditative experience for the soul.

Must-Do Activities

The number one activity here is to visit a local tea farmer. Many families open their homes and small plantations to visitors. You can sit in their rustic teahouses, learn about the process of making Longjing tea, and of course, taste and purchase it directly from the source. The hospitality is genuine, and the tea is sublime.

After your tea tasting, take a leisurely walk through the tea fields. The orderly rows of tea bushes create beautiful patterns on the landscape. The air is filled with the clean, fresh scent of tea leaves. For a slightly more structured hike, you can follow the paths that connect Meijiawu to the nearby Bamboo Lined Path at Yunqi, a stunning tunnel of bamboo that provides deep, cool shade.

Getting There & Pro Tip

A simple taxi or ride-share ride from anywhere in Hangzhou will get you to Meijiawu in under 40 minutes. Public buses also serve the area reliably. Pro Tip: Go on a weekday to avoid the weekend crowds. Don't be shy about walking a little further from the main village center to find a teahouse that feels more private and authentic. The experience of quiet conversation over multiple infusions of tea is the true treasure here.
