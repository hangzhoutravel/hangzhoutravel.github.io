Hangzhou’s Best Instagram Spots for Bubble Tea

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, a city where ancient charm meets modern vibes, is a paradise for bubble tea lovers and Instagram enthusiasts alike. From serene lakeside cafes to neon-lit urban hangouts, the city offers countless photogenic spots to sip, snap, and savor. Whether you’re a local or a traveler, these bubble tea hotspots will elevate your feed while satisfying your boba cravings.

1. West Lake Bubble Tea Bliss

Lakeside Sips with a View

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without visiting West Lake, and luckily, there are plenty of bubble tea shops nearby that offer stunning backdrops. HeyTea (喜茶) near the Broken Bridge serves artisanal teas with creamy cheese foam—perfect for a lakeside photo op. Pro tip: Grab your drink and stroll along the willow-lined paths for a dreamy shot.

Sunset Boba Sessions

For golden-hour lovers, Lelecha (乐乐茶) at Hubin Walk Street is a must. Their fruit-infused teas and fluffy milk bread pair beautifully with the sunset reflecting off the lake. Capture the moment with a boba cup in hand and the iconic Leifeng Pagoda in the distance.

2. Hefang Street’s Vintage Vibes

Retro Boba Cafés

Hefang Street, a historic pedestrian zone, is dotted with charming tea houses and trendy bubble tea joints. Machi Machi (麦吉) stands out with its minimalist decor and signature bottled milk tea—a favorite of Jay Chou fans. The rustic brick walls and hanging lanterns make for a nostalgic yet Insta-worthy setting.

Street-Style Snaps

Don’t miss Yidiandian (一点点), a budget-friendly spot where you can pose with your drink against the backdrop of traditional Chinese architecture. Try their classic pearl milk tea and snap a candid mid-sip shot under the red lanterns.

3. Qiantang River’s Modern Flair

Skyline and Sips

Head to the Qiantang River area for a futuristic bubble tea experience. Naixue Tea (奈雪の茶) in MixC Mall offers rooftop seating with panoramic river views. Their floral teas and soft-euro bread are as photogenic as the glittering cityscape behind you.

Neon Nights

For after-dark content, Chagee (霸王茶姬) near the Olympic Sports Center serves premium tea blends in sleek packaging. The neon lights of the riverfront skyline create a dazzling contrast—ideal for moody, high-fashion boba shots.

4. Xixi Wetland’s Hidden Gems

Nature-Meets-Boba

Escape the hustle at Xixi Wetland, where tranquil waterways and lush greenery set the scene. Tea’stone (茶颜悦色), a boutique tea house, blends Hangzhou’s tea culture with modern bubble tea twists. Their bamboo-themed cups look stunning against the wetland’s rustic bridges and lotus ponds.

Boat Ride Boba

For a unique angle, grab a drink from Coco (都可) and hop on a wooden boat. The reflection of your boba cup on the water is pure magic—just don’t spill!

5. In77’s Trendy Hotspots

Luxury Boba

Hangzhou’s premier shopping district, In77, is a goldmine for stylish bubble tea spots. Artea (ARTEASG) in Area C serves Insta-famous drinks like strawberry shortcake milk tea, complete with a mini dessert on top. The marble counters and chic interiors are a blogger’s dream.

Pop Culture Picks

K-pop fans flock to Tiger Sugar (老虎堂) for their caramel-laced “dirty boba.” The dripping syrup effect is a visual treat, especially when paired with In77’s vibrant murals and LED screens.

6. Lingyin Temple’s Serene Sips

Spiritual Refreshment

Near the famed Lingyin Temple, Daxi Tea (大溪地) offers zen-inspired drinks like matcha bubble tea with red bean. The tranquil courtyard, adorned with Buddhist motifs, adds a peaceful vibe to your photos.

Monastic Aesthetics

Sip slowly and capture the harmony of your drink beside ancient stone carvings—a rare blend of spirituality and boba culture.

7. Wulin Square’s Urban Energy

High-Tech Boba

Wulin Square’s Happy Lemon (快乐柠檬) stands out with its robot-assisted drink-making. Film the futuristic process and toast to innovation with their salted cheese tea.

Night Market Feels

After dark, the square transforms into a foodie haven. Pair your bubble tea from Shuyi Tealicious (书亦烧仙草) with street snacks for a lively, colorful feed update.

Hangzhou’s bubble tea scene is as diverse as its landscapes—each sip comes with a story and a stunning backdrop. So charge your phone, clear your camera roll, and get ready to drink (and post) your way through the city!
