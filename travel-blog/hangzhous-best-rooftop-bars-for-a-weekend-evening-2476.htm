Hangzhou’s Best Rooftop Bars for a Weekend Evening

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

There’s something undeniably magical about sipping a cocktail while overlooking a city’s skyline as the sun dips below the horizon. In Hangzhou, where ancient charm meets modern sophistication, rooftop bars offer the perfect blend of ambiance, views, and vibes for a memorable weekend evening. Whether you’re a traveler seeking a taste of local nightlife or a resident looking for a new spot to unwind, these elevated venues promise an unforgettable experience.

The Allure of Hangzhou’s Rooftop Scene

Hangzhou, famed for its serene West Lake and lush tea plantations, might not be the first city that comes to mind for a buzzing nightlife. But don’t let its tranquil daytime vibe fool you—when the sun sets, the city’s rooftop bars come alive with stylish crowds, creative mixology, and panoramic views that rival any global metropolis.

From sleek, modern lounges to hidden gems with a bohemian twist, here’s your guide to the best rooftop bars in Hangzhou for a weekend evening.

1. Cloud 9 at the Hangzhou Marriott Hotel Qianjiang

Location: 15F, Hangzhou Marriott Hotel Qianjiang

Perched high above the Qianjiang New Town, Cloud 9 offers a bird’s-eye view of Hangzhou’s futuristic skyline, including the iconic Hangzhou Olympic Sports Center. This sleek, contemporary bar is a favorite among both locals and international visitors for its sophisticated atmosphere and expertly crafted cocktails.

Why Visit?
- Stunning Views: Floor-to-ceiling windows frame the Qianjiang River and the city’s glittering lights.
- Signature Drinks: Try the "West Lake Breeze," a refreshing gin-based cocktail infused with local Longjing tea.
- Live Music: Weekend evenings often feature smooth jazz or acoustic sets.

Pro Tip: Arrive before sunset to snag a window seat and watch the city transition from day to night.

2. The Moon & The Desert

Location: 7F, 18 Nanshan Road

Tucked away in the heart of Hangzhou’s historic Nanshan Road, The Moon & The Desert is a bohemian-inspired rooftop bar that feels like a secret garden in the sky. With its eclectic decor, hanging plants, and mismatched vintage furniture, this spot exudes a laid-back, artsy vibe.

Why Visit?
- Unique Ambiance: Think fairy lights, reclaimed wood, and a cozy, intimate setting.
- Creative Cocktails: Their "Tea-Infused Negroni" is a must-try, blending classic Italian bitters with Hangzhou’s famed tea flavors.
- Local Artisans: The bar frequently hosts pop-up events featuring local artists and musicians.

Pro Tip: Ask the bartender for their off-menu specials—they love surprising guests with personalized creations.

3. Xanadu Rooftop Bar

Location: 12F, 288 Yan’an Road

For those who love a touch of glamour, Xanadu Rooftop Bar delivers with its chic, Miami-inspired decor and vibrant energy. Located in the bustling Yan’an Road district, this hotspot is perfect for groups looking to dance under the stars.

Why Visit?
- Party Vibes: DJs spin house and electronic beats on weekends, drawing a lively crowd.
- Instagram-Worthy Setting: Neon lights, palm trees, and a glass-bottom pool make for killer photo ops.
- Asian Fusion Bites: Their small plates, like truffle dumplings and spicy tuna tartare, pair perfectly with cocktails.

Pro Tip: Reserve a cabana in advance if you’re with a group—it’s the best way to enjoy the party in style.

4. The Quiet Quarter

Location: 5F, 31 Pinghai Road

True to its name, The Quiet Quarter offers a more subdued, refined rooftop experience. Overlooking West Lake, this bar is ideal for those who prefer conversation over clubbing. The minimalist design, with clean lines and neutral tones, lets the natural beauty of the surroundings take center stage.

Why Visit?
- West Lake Views: Few spots offer such an unobstructed, peaceful view of the lake.
- Wine Focus: Their curated wine list features rare vintages from France and Ningxia.
- Seasonal Menus: Cocktails change with the seasons, incorporating local ingredients like osmanthus and lychee.

Pro Tip: Visit in autumn for the best views—the lake is framed by golden gingko trees.

5. Above the Fog at Hyatt Regency Hangzhou

Location: 28F, Hyatt Regency Hangzhou

For a truly elevated experience, head to Above the Fog, where you’ll literally find yourself above the city’s misty skyline. This high-end rooftop bar is all about luxury, with plush seating, impeccable service, and a menu that’s as impressive as the view.

Why Visit?
- 360-Degree Views: The open-air terrace provides unparalleled vistas of Hangzhou’s mountains and skyscrapers.
- Craft Cocktails: Their "Dragon Well Old Fashioned" is a smoky, tea-infused twist on the classic.
- Exclusive Atmosphere: The dress code is smart casual, making it a great spot for a date night.

Pro Tip: If you’re celebrating a special occasion, let the staff know—they often go above and beyond with personalized touches.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

While the above spots are well-known, Hangzhou’s rooftop bar scene also has a few under-the-radar treasures worth exploring.

The Rooftop at The Wyndham Grand Plaza Royale

A newcomer to the scene, this bar offers a more intimate setting with a focus on craft spirits. Their "Hangzhou Mule," made with bamboo-infused vodka, is a standout.

Lazy Lounge

True to its name, this spot is all about kicking back with a beer and enjoying the breeze. It’s less about fancy cocktails and more about good company and relaxed vibes.

Final Thoughts for Your Weekend Adventure

Hangzhou’s rooftop bars are as diverse as the city itself, offering everything from high-energy parties to serene, wine-sipping retreats. No matter which one you choose, you’re guaranteed a weekend evening filled with great drinks, breathtaking views, and the kind of memories that linger long after the last sip. So grab your friends (or a good book), head to one of these elevated havens, and toast to the beauty of Hangzhou from above.
