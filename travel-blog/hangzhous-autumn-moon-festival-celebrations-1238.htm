Hangzhou’s Autumn Moon Festival Celebrations

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

The Autumn Moon Festival, also known as the Mid-Autumn Festival, is one of China’s most enchanting cultural events, and Hangzhou—with its poetic West Lake and rich traditions—hosts some of the most unforgettable celebrations. If you’re planning a trip to this picturesque city during the festival, prepare for moonlit boat rides, delectable mooncakes, and a vibrant blend of ancient and modern festivities.

Why Hangzhou is the Perfect Destination for the Autumn Moon Festival

Hangzhou’s deep connection to the Mid-Autumn Festival dates back centuries. The city’s West Lake, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, becomes the focal point of celebrations, where the moon’s reflection shimmers on the water, creating a scene straight out of a classical Chinese painting.

The Legend of the Moon Goddess and Hangzhou

Local folklore ties Hangzhou to the myth of Chang’e, the Moon Goddess. According to legend, she once descended to West Lake, leaving behind an aura of magic that lingers to this day. During the festival, lantern-lit performances and storytelling sessions bring this tale to life, making it a must-see for visitors.

Top Autumn Moon Festival Experiences in Hangzhou

1. Moonlit Boat Rides on West Lake

No visit to Hangzhou during the Mid-Autumn Festival is complete without a nighttime cruise on West Lake. Traditional wooden boats adorned with lanterns glide across the water, offering unparalleled views of the full moon. Some tours even include live guzheng (a traditional Chinese zither) performances, adding to the ethereal atmosphere.

Pro Tip: Book your boat ride in advance—this is one of the most sought-after experiences during the festival.

2. The Legendary Mooncakes of Hangzhou

Hangzhou’s mooncakes are a culinary highlight. Unlike the more common Cantonese-style mooncakes, Hangzhou’s versions often feature delicate, flaky crusts with fillings like sweet osmanthus, lotus seed paste, and locally sourced nuts.

Where to Try Them:
- Fang Hui Fang – A historic teahouse famous for its traditional mooncakes.
- Zhi Wei Guan – Known for its innovative flavors, including green tea and red bean.

3. Lantern Displays at Hefang Street

Hefang Street, Hangzhou’s ancient pedestrian thoroughfare, transforms into a dazzling lantern wonderland during the festival. Intricately crafted lanterns depicting mythical creatures, flowers, and even scenes from Chinese opera line the streets. Visitors can also participate in lantern-making workshops or enjoy street performances.

4. The Three Pools Mirroring the Moon

One of West Lake’s iconic sights, the Three Pools Mirroring the Moon, is especially mesmerizing during the festival. The three small pagodas in the lake are lit with candles, creating an illusion of multiple moons dancing on the water’s surface. It’s a photographer’s paradise.

Modern Twists on Traditional Celebrations

While Hangzhou honors its ancient customs, the city also embraces contemporary ways to celebrate the Autumn Moon Festival.

5. Digital Light Shows at Qianjiang New Town

For those who love a mix of tradition and modernity, Qianjiang New Town hosts a spectacular digital light show. Projections of the moon, animated renditions of Chang’e, and laser displays illuminate the skyline, offering a futuristic take on the festival.

6. Moon-Themed Cafés and Pop-Ups

Hangzhou’s trendy cafés and bars join in the festivities with limited-edition mooncake lattes, osmanthus-infused cocktails, and Instagram-worthy dessert platters. Check out:
- Starbucks Reserve Hangzhou – Special mooncake-flavored coffee blends.
- Xixi Wetland Teahouses – Moonlight tea ceremonies with a view.

Practical Tips for Travelers

Best Time to Visit

The Autumn Moon Festival follows the lunar calendar, usually falling in September or early October. The celebrations last for about three days, with the most vibrant events happening on the actual night of the full moon.

Where to Stay

  • Luxury: Amanfayun – A serene retreat near Lingyin Temple.
  • Boutique: The Azure Qiantang – Offers moon-viewing terraces.
  • Budget: Hangzhou Mingtown International Youth Hostel – Great for solo travelers.

Getting Around

Hangzhou’s metro and bike-sharing systems make it easy to explore. However, expect crowds during the festival, so plan accordingly.

Hidden Gems for an Authentic Experience

7. Tea Harvesting in Longjing Village

The festival coincides with the autumn tea harvest. Join a tea-picking session in Longjing Village, followed by a moonlit tea-tasting session with local farmers.

8. Night Hikes at Beishan Road

For a quieter celebration, hike up Beishan Road for a panoramic view of West Lake under the moonlight. Locals often gather here for picnics and poetry recitals.

Final Thoughts

Hangzhou’s Autumn Moon Festival is a sensory feast—where history, nature, and modern flair come together under the glow of the harvest moon. Whether you’re savoring a mooncake by the lake or drifting on a lantern-lit boat, the magic of this festival will stay with you long after you leave.
