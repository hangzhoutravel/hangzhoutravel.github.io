Hangzhou Itinerary: Best Places for a Leisurely Stroll

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, often dubbed "Paradise on Earth," is a city that effortlessly blends natural beauty with rich cultural heritage. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, the city’s serene lakes, ancient temples, and charming tea villages offer endless opportunities for a leisurely stroll. Here’s a curated itinerary to help you discover the best spots for a relaxing walk in Hangzhou.

West Lake (Xi Hu): The Heart of Hangzhou

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without a visit to West Lake, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This iconic lake is surrounded by pagodas, willow trees, and lotus-filled ponds, making it the perfect place for a peaceful stroll.

Best Walking Routes Around West Lake

  1. Bai Causeway (Bai Di)
    Stretching nearly a mile, this scenic path connects the Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao) to Solitary Hill (Gu Shan). The causeway is lined with peach trees and weeping willows, offering stunning views of the lake.

  2. Su Causeway (Su Di)
    Named after the famous poet Su Dongpo, this route is especially beautiful in spring when cherry blossoms bloom. Stop by the Viewing Fish at Flower Harbor garden for a tranquil break.

  3. Lingering Snow on Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao Can Xue)
    A poetic spot that’s even more magical in winter when light snow dusts the bridge. It’s a favorite among photographers and romantics.

Hidden Gems Near West Lake

  • Guozhuang Garden: A lesser-known classical garden with intricate pavilions and quiet corners.
  • Turtle Pond (Yu Qian Ci): A secluded area with a serene pond and lush greenery.

Xixi Wetland Park: A Nature Lover’s Retreat

For those who prefer a slower pace, Xixi Wetland Park is a sprawling network of waterways, wooden walkways, and traditional villages. It’s one of the few remaining urban wetlands in China.

What to Explore in Xixi Wetland

  • Boat Rides: Glide through narrow canals while admiring the water lilies and rustic bridges.
  • Ancient Villages: Visit Shen Zhuang and Zhou Jia Cun to see well-preserved Qing Dynasty architecture.
  • Plum Blossom Avenue: In late winter, this path becomes a fragrant wonderland.

Hefang Street: A Stroll Through History

If you love combining walks with shopping and snacks, Hefang Street is a must-visit. This pedestrian-only street is lined with traditional shops, tea houses, and street food stalls.

Highlights of Hefang Street

  • Hu Qing Yu Tang: A centuries-old traditional Chinese medicine pharmacy turned museum.
  • Dough Figurine Artisans: Watch skilled craftsmen shape colorful dough into lifelike figures.
  • Hangzhou Cuisine: Try local favorites like Dongpo Pork and West Lake Vinegar Fish.

Longjing Tea Village: A Walk Among Tea Fields

Hangzhou is famous for its Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, and a visit to the Longjing Tea Plantations is a sensory delight. The rolling green hills and fresh mountain air make it ideal for a rejuvenating walk.

Tips for Visiting Longjing

  • Hike to Lion Peak (Shi Feng): The highest point offers panoramic views of the tea terraces.
  • Tea Tasting: Stop by a local farmhouse to sip freshly brewed Longjing tea.
  • Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies (Jiu Xi Shi Ba Jian): A shaded trail that winds through bamboo forests and streams.

Lingyin Temple & Feilai Feng: A Spiritual Stroll

For a mix of nature and spirituality, head to Lingyin Temple, one of China’s most important Buddhist temples. The adjacent Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar) is dotted with ancient rock carvings.

Walking Routes in the Area

  • Lingyin Temple Complex: Wander through grand halls and admire the 1,700-year-old statues.
  • Feilai Feng Grottoes: Follow the stone paths to discover hundreds of intricate Buddhist carvings.
  • Beigao Peak: A quieter hiking trail with sweeping views of Hangzhou.

Qianjiang New Town: A Modern Contrast

If you want a break from traditional scenery, Qianjiang New Town offers sleek skyscrapers, waterfront promenades, and futuristic architecture.

Best Spots for a Leisurely Walk

  • Qianjiang Century City Park: A sprawling green space with futuristic light displays at night.
  • Hangzhou Grand Theatre: An architectural marvel shaped like a lotus flower.
  • Qianjiang River Walk: Perfect for an evening stroll with dazzling city views.

Final Tips for Your Hangzhou Stroll

  • Best Time to Walk: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.
  • Footwear: Comfortable shoes are a must, especially for tea village hikes.
  • Local Etiquette: Respect quiet zones near temples and tea plantations.

Hangzhou’s charm lies in its ability to make every stroll feel like a journey through time—whether you’re tracing the footsteps of ancient poets or soaking in modern urban vibes. So lace up your walking shoes and let the city’s beauty unfold at your own pace.
