The Ideal 5-Day Hangzhou Itinerary for Families

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, often referred to as "Heaven on Earth," is a perfect destination for families seeking a mix of nature, culture, and adventure. With its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich history, this city offers something for everyone. Below is a carefully crafted 5-day itinerary that balances relaxation, exploration, and fun for all ages.

Day 1: Exploring West Lake and Its Surroundings

Morning: Leifeng Pagoda and West Lake Cruise

Start your trip with a visit to Leifeng Pagoda, a historic tower offering panoramic views of West Lake. Kids will love the legend of the White Snake associated with this site. Afterward, hop on a traditional boat cruise to soak in the lake’s beauty. Opt for a hand-rowed boat for a more intimate experience.

Afternoon: Stroll Along Su Causeway

Walk or rent bikes to explore Su Causeway, a scenic path lined with willow trees and blooming flowers. Stop by the West Lake Museum to learn about the lake’s history through interactive exhibits—great for curious young minds.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

Cap off the day with the Impression West Lake Show, a breathtaking outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou. The combination of lights, music, and dance on the water will mesmerize the whole family.

Day 2: Tea Culture and Nature Adventures

Morning: Dragon Well Tea Plantation

Head to the Dragon Well Tea Plantation (Longjing) for a hands-on tea-picking experience. Families can learn about tea processing and enjoy a tasting session. The rolling green hills make for fantastic photo ops!

Afternoon: Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies

Hike the Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies trail, a shaded, kid-friendly path with streams and bamboo forests. Pack a picnic and take breaks at the charming stone bridges along the way.

Evening: Hefang Street

Explore Hefang Street, a bustling pedestrian lane filled with traditional snacks, handicrafts, and street performances. Let the kids try local treats like sticky rice cakes and sweet osmanthus jelly.

Day 3: Historical Hangzhou and Amusement Fun

Morning: Lingyin Temple and Feilai Feng Grottoes

Visit Lingyin Temple, one of China’s most famous Buddhist temples. The nearby Feilai Feng Grottoes feature hundreds of ancient rock carvings—kids will enjoy spotting the intricate statues hidden in the cliffs.

Afternoon: Hangzhou Songcheng Park

Spend the afternoon at Hangzhou Songcheng Park, a theme park that recreates the Song Dynasty era. Don’t miss the "Romance of the Song Dynasty" show and the interactive street performances. The park’s ghost house and mirror maze are big hits with older kids.

Evening: Qinghefang Night Market

Return to Qinghefang Ancient Street for evening shopping. Pick up souvenirs like silk scarves or hand-painted fans while enjoying the lively atmosphere.

Day 4: Water Towns and Modern Hangzhou

Morning: Wuzhen Water Town (Day Trip)

Take a short trip to Wuzhen, a picturesque canal town. Wander the cobblestone streets, ride a gondola, and visit traditional workshops where kids can see silk weaving and indigo dyeing in action.

Afternoon: Hangzhou Grand Canal

Back in Hangzhou, explore the Grand Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visit the China Umbrella Museum or China Knife, Scissors, and Sword Museum—both quirky and educational.

Evening: Qianjiang New City Light Show

Head to Qianjiang New City for a dazzling light show along the river. The futuristic skyline contrasts beautifully with Hangzhou’s ancient charm.

Day 5: Relaxation and Farewell to Hangzhou

Morning: Xixi Wetland Park

Spend your last day at Xixi Wetland Park, a tranquil network of waterways. Take a boat ride, spot wildlife, and let the kids run free in the open spaces.

Afternoon: Shopping at In77

For some final retail therapy, visit In77, Hangzhou’s premier shopping district. From high-end brands to quirky local stores, there’s something for every taste.

Evening: Sunset at Baochu Pagoda

End your trip with a hike up to Baochu Pagoda for a stunning sunset over the city. It’s the perfect way to reflect on your family’s unforgettable Hangzhou adventure.

This itinerary ensures a balanced mix of education, excitement, and relaxation, making it ideal for families. Whether you’re sipping tea in the plantations or cruising through ancient water towns, Hangzhou promises memories that will last a lifetime.
