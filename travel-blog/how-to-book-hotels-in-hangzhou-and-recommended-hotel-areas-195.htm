How to Book Hotels in Hangzhou and Recommended Hotel Areas

Home / Travel Blog

Hangzhou, known for its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and cultural heritage, is one of China’s most popular tourist destinations. Whether you're visiting to explore the iconic West Lake, experience the serene tea plantations, or delve into the bustling modern city life, choosing the right hotel is crucial for a comfortable and enjoyable stay. This guide will help you understand how to book hotels in Hangzhou and highlight the best areas for accommodations.

Booking Hotels in Hangzhou

Online Booking Platforms

One of the easiest and most convenient ways to book hotels in Hangzhou is through online platforms. Websites and apps like Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda, and Trip.com offer a wide selection of accommodations ranging from budget hostels to luxury resorts.

  • Advantages: These platforms often provide competitive prices, discounts, and reviews from previous guests. You can filter options by location, price, amenities, and rating.
  • Mobile Payments: Many platforms accept international credit cards and popular payment methods like Alipay and WeChat Pay, making the booking process seamless for both domestic and international travelers.

Hotel Websites and Direct Bookings

For travelers who prefer direct communication, many hotels in Hangzhou have official websites where you can book rooms. Direct bookings sometimes come with exclusive perks, such as free breakfast or flexible cancellation policies.

  • Pro Tip: If you have a specific hotel chain membership, you can earn points or benefits by booking directly through the hotel’s website.

Local Agencies and Tour Packages

Local travel agencies in Hangzhou also offer hotel booking services, often as part of a package deal. These packages may include guided tours, transportation, and meals.

  • Who Is It For?: This option is ideal for those who prefer a hassle-free experience or are visiting Hangzhou for the first time.

Booking Upon Arrival

If you’re traveling during the off-season, booking a hotel upon arrival can be an option. However, during peak tourist seasons like spring (cherry blossom season) and autumn (golden weeks), it’s strongly recommended to book in advance to secure your preferred accommodation.

Recommended Hotel Areas in Hangzhou

1. West Lake Area (Xihu)

Why Stay Here?
The West Lake area is the most iconic and picturesque location in Hangzhou. Staying near the lake allows you to enjoy stunning sunrise and sunset views, serene walking paths, and convenient access to popular attractions like the Broken Bridge, Leifeng Pagoda, and Lingyin Temple.

  • Accommodation Options:

    • Luxury: Banyan Tree Hangzhou, Four Seasons Hangzhou at West Lake
    • Mid-range: The Eyrie Boutique Hotel
    • Budget: Westlake Youth Hostel

  • Activities: Boat rides, biking around the lake, and exploring historic landmarks.

Who Is It For?
Perfect for first-time visitors, nature lovers, and those seeking a tranquil atmosphere.

2. Wulin Square and Downtown Hangzhou

Why Stay Here?
Wulin Square is the commercial and cultural hub of Hangzhou. This area is bustling with shopping malls, restaurants, and entertainment venues. It’s also well-connected to public transportation, making it easy to explore other parts of the city.

  • Accommodation Options:

    • Luxury: The Ritz-Carlton, Hangzhou Marriott Hotel Qianjiang
    • Mid-range: Holiday Inn Express Hangzhou Huanglong
    • Budget: Ibis Hangzhou Wulin Square Hotel

  • Activities: Shopping at Wulin Square, visiting the Zhejiang Museum of Natural History, and enjoying the vibrant nightlife.

Who Is It For?
Ideal for business travelers, shopaholics, and those who enjoy the convenience of city life.

3. Qianjiang New Town

Why Stay Here?
Qianjiang New Town is Hangzhou’s modern financial district, known for its futuristic skyline and luxurious accommodations. The area is home to the iconic Qianjiang Century City and the stunning Hangzhou Grand Theater.

  • Accommodation Options:

    • Luxury: InterContinental Hangzhou, Conrad Hangzhou
    • Mid-range: Holiday Inn Hangzhou CBD
    • Budget: Qianjiang Boutique Guesthouse

  • Activities: Admire the light show along the Qiantang River and explore contemporary art at the Grand Theater.

Who Is It For?
Perfect for business travelers and those who prefer a modern, urban vibe.

4. Binjiang District

Why Stay Here?
Binjiang District is a quieter area south of the Qiantang River, offering a mix of modern amenities and scenic riverside views. It’s less crowded than the city center but still provides easy access to major attractions.

  • Accommodation Options:

    • Luxury: Sheraton Grand Hangzhou Binjiang Hotel
    • Mid-range: Hampton by Hilton Hangzhou Binjiang
    • Budget: Binjiang Business Inn

  • Activities: Riverside walks, exploring tech hubs, and enjoying local dining options.

Who Is It For?
A great choice for families and travelers seeking a peaceful yet convenient location.

5. Xiasha District

Why Stay Here?
Xiasha is an up-and-coming area popular among young travelers and students, thanks to its proximity to Hangzhou’s universities and tech parks. The district offers affordable accommodations and a vibrant atmosphere.

  • Accommodation Options:

    • Luxury: DoubleTree by Hilton Hangzhou East
    • Mid-range: Ramada Plaza Hangzhou Xiaoshan
    • Budget: Xiasha Youth Hostel

  • Activities: Explore local cafes, attend cultural events, and visit nearby parks.

Who Is It For?
Budget travelers, students, and those exploring the city’s modern development.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hotel

Consider Your Itinerary

If your main goal is to explore West Lake, staying in the Xihu area will save you travel time. For a mix of sightseeing and shopping, the downtown area is a better option.

Check Reviews and Ratings

Always read guest reviews on platforms like TripAdvisor or Booking.com to get insights into the hotel’s service quality, cleanliness, and location.

Look for Amenities

Ensure the hotel offers essential amenities like free Wi-Fi, breakfast options, and shuttle services. For families, check if the hotel has child-friendly facilities.

Transportation Accessibility

Choose a hotel near metro stations or major bus routes for convenience. This is especially important if you plan to visit attractions in different parts of Hangzhou.

Best Time to Book Hotels in Hangzhou

Peak Seasons

Hangzhou is most crowded during spring and autumn, particularly during cherry blossom season (March to April) and the Mid-Autumn Festival. If you’re traveling during these times, book your hotel at least 2–3 months in advance.

Off-Peak Seasons

Winter (December to February) and the summer months (July to August) are less crowded, and hotel prices tend to drop. This is a great time to find deals and enjoy a quieter experience.

Hangzhou offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. By planning ahead and choosing the right location, you can enhance your travel experience and fully enjoy the beauty and culture of this historic city. Whether you prefer lakeside luxury, modern city vibes, or budget-friendly options, Hangzhou has something for everyone.
