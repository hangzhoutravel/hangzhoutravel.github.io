How to Get a Hangzhou Travel Card: A Step-by-Step Guide

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China’s most picturesque cities. Famous for its West Lake, ancient tea culture, and modern tech hubs like Alibaba’s headquarters, it’s a must-visit for travelers. To make your trip smoother, the Hangzhou Travel Card (or Hangzhou Tong) is an essential tool for accessing public transport, scenic spots, and even discounts at local businesses. Here’s everything you need to know about getting one.

Why You Need a Hangzhou Travel Card

Before diving into the how, let’s talk about the why. The Hangzhou Travel Card isn’t just a transit pass—it’s a key to unlocking the city’s conveniences:

  • Seamless Public Transport: Use it on buses, metro lines, taxis, and even water buses on the Grand Canal.
  • Discounted Entry: Many attractions, like West Lake’s Leifeng Pagoda or Lingyin Temple, offer reduced fees for cardholders.
  • Cashless Convenience: Avoid fumbling for change—just tap and go.
  • Local Perks: Some cafes, museums, and shops give exclusive deals to card users.

Types of Hangzhou Travel Cards

Hangzhou offers several card options tailored to different needs:

1. Standard Hangzhou Tong Card

The all-purpose card for daily commuters and tourists. It’s rechargeable and works across most transport systems.

2. Tourist-Specific Cards

  • 24/48/72-Hour Passes: Unlimited rides within the chosen timeframe, plus bundled scenic spot discounts.
  • West Lake Cultural Heritage Card: Combines transport with entry to key cultural sites.

3. Mobile Virtual Cards

For tech-savvy travelers, apps like Alipay or WeChat offer digital versions linked to your phone.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Your Card

Step 1: Choose Your Card Type

Decide whether you need a physical card (ideal for longer stays) or a digital one (for short trips).

Step 2: Where to Buy

Physical Cards:

  • Metro Stations: Most stations, especially at airports (Xiaoshan International) or major hubs like Longxiangqiao, have vending machines or service counters.
  • Convenience Stores: Look for FamilyMart or Kedi outlets displaying the Hangzhou Tong logo.
  • Tourist Centers: The West Lake Visitor Center or Hangzhou Railway Station often sell specialized tourist cards.

Digital Cards:

  • Alipay: Search for “Hangzhou Metro” or “Hangzhou Public Transport” in the app to activate a virtual card.
  • WeChat: Similar to Alipay, link your card via the City Services feature.

Step 3: Load Money or Activate

  • Physical Cards: Pay a ¥20 deposit (refundable upon return) and load credit (¥50–500). Machines accept cash/WeChat/Alipay.
  • Digital Cards: Top up via your linked payment method.

Step 4: Start Using It

  • Public Transport: Tap on bus/metro card readers.
  • Attractions: Show your card at ticket counters for discounts.

Pro Tips for Maximizing Your Card

1. Check for Limited-Time Promotions

During festivals like the Mid-Autumn Festival or Hangzhou International Tea Expo, the city often rolls out special card perks (e.g., free metro rides after 7 PM).

2. Combine with Other Passes

Pair your card with the Yangtze Delta Pass to use it in nearby cities like Shanghai or Suzhou.

3. Monitor Your Balance

Recharge at metro stations or via apps. Low balance? A single red light flash at the card reader will warn you.

4. Keep It Safe

While digital cards can’t be lost, physical ones aren’t replaceable if damaged. Consider a protective sleeve.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

"My Card Isn’t Working!"

  • Low Balance: Recharge immediately.
  • Damaged Card: Visit a service center (e.g., Ding’an Road Metro Station) for assistance.

"Can I Get a Refund?"

Yes! Return your physical card (with receipt) to designated spots for a deposit refund. Unused credit is refundable too.

"Is There an English Helpline?"

Call +86 571 12345 for limited English support, or ask for help at hotel concierge desks.

Beyond Transport: Hidden Uses of the Card

  • Bike Sharing: Scan QR codes on Hangzhou’s public bikes for discounted rides.
  • Library Access: Some versions grant entry to Hangzhou’s futuristic Zhejiang Library.
  • Museum VIP Lines: Skip queues at the China National Tea Museum by flashing your card.

Final Notes

The Hangzhou Travel Card is a small investment for a vastly smoother trip. Whether you’re cruising the Grand Canal, exploring Hefang Street’s snacks, or commuting to a tech conference, this little card is your golden ticket.

Now, go grab yours—and enjoy Hangzhou like a local!
