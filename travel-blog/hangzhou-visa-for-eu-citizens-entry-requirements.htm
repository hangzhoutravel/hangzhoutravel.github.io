Hangzhou Visa for EU Citizens: Entry Requirements

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

For European travelers, the allure of China often begins with the Great Wall or the Terracotta Warriors, but those in the know understand that the true soul of classical China resides in Hangzhou. A city that Marco Polo once described as "the finest and most splendid city in the world," Hangzhou is a mesmerizing blend of ancient poetry, serene landscapes, and cutting-edge modernity. As an EU citizen, the journey to this paradise starts with understanding the entry requirements. While the process might seem daunting at first, it's a straightforward path that leads to an unforgettable experience. This guide will navigate you through the visa process while painting a picture of the incredible sights that await.

Do EU Citizens Need a Visa for Hangzhou?

The short and most common answer is yes. China requires a visa for most EU passport holders for tourism purposes. Hangzhou, as a major city within China, falls under this national regulation. You will need to apply for a standard Chinese Tourist Visa (L Visa) before your departure.

However, there is a fantastic and often underutilized exception that can make a short trip incredibly convenient: the

144-Hour Visa-Free Transit Policy

This is a golden ticket for travelers looking to sample Hangzhou without the full visa application process. Here’s how it works:

If you are transiting through China on your way to a third country (or region), you can stay in the Hangzhou area for up to 144 hours (6 days) without a visa. The key is that you must enter and exit from a specific designated region. Hangzhou is part of the Yangtze River Delta region, which includes Shanghai, Jiangsu, and Zhejiang provinces.

For example, you could: * Fly into Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG) and declare your 144-hour transit. * Take the high-speed train (a mere 45-minute journey) to Hangzhou. * Explore Hangzhou and its surroundings for up to 6 days. * Depart from the same region, for instance, flying out of Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport (HGH) to your final destination (e.g., Thailand or Japan).

This policy is perfect for a focused trip to eastern China. Just ensure your passports are valid, you have confirmed onward tickets to a third country, and you fill out the required forms upon arrival.

The Standard Chinese Tourist Visa (L Visa) Application Process

For longer stays or itineraries that don't fit the transit rules, the Tourist Visa is your route. The process is standardized across Chinese consulates.

Required Documents Checklist

Gathering your documents is the most critical step. Here’s what you’ll need:

  1. Passport: Your original passport with at least six months of remaining validity and at least two blank visa pages. Include a photocopy of the bio-data page.
  2. Visa Application Form: The fully completed and truthfully answered "Visa Application Form of the People's Republic of China." This is done online on the Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) website for your country. Print it out and sign it.
  3. Photo: One recent, color passport-sized photo (48mm x 33mm) with a light background, glued to the application form.
  4. Proof of Legal Residence: If you are applying in an EU country where you are not a citizen (e.g., a French citizen applying in Germany), you must provide your valid residence permit.
  5. Round-Trip Air Ticket & Hotel Bookings: You need to provide confirmed flight reservations and proof of hotel bookings for your entire stay in China. If staying with friends, you need an invitation letter.
  6. Itinerary in China: A simple day-by-day plan of your trip (e.g., Day 1: West Lake, Day 2: Lingyin Temple).
  7. Financial Proof: Recent bank statements (usually from the last 3 months) demonstrating you have sufficient funds to cover your trip.

Where and How to Apply

You cannot apply directly to the Chinese embassy or consulate in most European countries. You must submit your application through a designated Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC). You can find the center nearest to you through their website. You typically need to book an appointment online, submit your paperwork in person, and then pick up your passport once the visa is processed. Processing usually takes about 4 to 5 business days, with expedited options available for an extra fee.

Visa Fees and Validity

The fee varies depending on your nationality and the number of entries you request. A standard single-entry tourist visa for most EU citizens costs around €100-€150. You can request: * Single-entry: Valid for 3 months from the date of issue. * Double-entry: Valid for 6 months. * Multiple-entry: Valid for 6, 12, or 24 months, often granted to frequent travelers.

Ensure you request the validity and entry type that best suits your travel plans.

Beyond the Visa: Experiencing Hangzhou's Tourism Hotspots

Securing your visa is the key that unlocks a treasure chest of experiences. Hangzhou is far more than a stop on a map; it's a feeling.

The Iconic West Lake: A UNESCO World Heritage Site

This is the heart and soul of Hangzhou. West Lake is not just a body of water; it's a work of art meticulously crafted by poets, painters, and emperors over centuries. The best way to experience it is by walking or cycling along its shores. Don't miss the "Ten Scenes of West Lake," which include: * Spring Dawn at Su Causeway: A beautiful causeway built by the famous poet Su Shi, perfect for a morning stroll. * Lotus in the Breeze at Crooked Courtyard: Visit in summer to see a breathtaking expanse of blooming lotus flowers. * Three Ponds Mirroring the Moon: Seen on the back of the Chinese 1 RMB note, these three small pagodas are iconic. * Evening Bell Ringing at Nanping Hill: A serene experience, especially as the day ends.

Take a boat ride to the middle of the lake to truly appreciate its scale and beauty. The view of the skyline with rolling hills in the background is unforgettable.

Ancient Temples and Spiritual Sanctuaries

  • Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul's Retreat): One of China's most important Zen Buddhist temples, this complex is a masterpiece. Explore the main halls with their magnificent statues, but don't overlook the real highlight: the Feilai Feng grottos. These limestone cliffs are covered in hundreds of ancient Buddhist rock carvings, some dating back to the 10th century.
  • Leifeng Pagoda: This pagoda, originally built in 975 AD, offers the most spectacular panoramic view of West Lake. Although the original collapsed, the current reconstruction houses the old foundations and is equipped with modern elevators for easy access.

A Taste of Hangzhou: The Birthplace of Dragon Well Tea

No trip to Hangzhou is complete without delving into its tea culture. The city is the home of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, one of China's most famous green teas. Take a trip to the Longjing Tea Plantations in the hills surrounding West Lake. You can walk among the terraced fields, visit a local farmhouse for a tea tasting session, and learn how to properly brew and appreciate this delicate tea. It’s a sensory and cultural immersion.

The Historic Hefang Street

For a taste of old Hangzhou, take a walk down this pedestrianized street. It's a reconstructed Song Dynasty-style street bustling with activity. You can find traditional handicrafts, sample local snacks like Dongpo Pork and Beggar's Chicken, watch sugar art being made, and absorb the lively atmosphere.

The Modern Side: Qianjiang New Town

Hangzhou isn't just about history. Across the Qiantang River from West Lake, you'll find a stunning modern central business district. The skyline, especially at night, is illuminated with dazzling lights. The G20 Summit was held here in 2016, and the city built impressive structures like the "Sun and Moon" buildings (Hangzhou International Conference Center and Hangzhou Grand Theatre) for the event. It’s a powerful contrast to the classical beauty of West Lake and a testament to China's rapid development.

Preparing the correct visa paperwork is the first step on your adventure. It’s a small investment of time for the reward of cycling around West Lake at dusk, sipping Dragon Well tea at its source, and standing in awe before ancient stone carvings. Hangzhou is a city that captures the harmonious spirit of China, and it’s waiting for you to explore.
