Where to Buy Souvenirs in Hangzhou: A Guide to the Best Shops and Markets

Home / Travel Blog

Hangzhou, a city renowned for its picturesque landscapes, historic sites, and cultural significance, is also a fantastic place to shop for unique souvenirs. From traditional Chinese handicrafts to modern art-inspired trinkets, Hangzhou offers a wide range of options for travelers looking to bring home a piece of this enchanting city. Here’s a detailed guide to the best places in Hangzhou to find souvenirs that capture the essence of this vibrant city.

1. Hefang Street

Hefang Street is undoubtedly one of the most popular destinations for souvenir shopping in Hangzhou. This historic pedestrian street is lined with traditional-style buildings and offers a variety of shops selling everything from tea and silk to handcrafted items and snacks.

What to Look For:

  • Longjing Tea: As one of Hangzhou’s most famous exports, Longjing (Dragon Well) tea is a must-buy. Many tea shops along Hefang Street offer tastings, so you can select your favorite.
  • Hangzhou Silk: Known for its softness and quality, Hangzhou silk is a classic souvenir. You can find silk scarves, clothing, and decorative items.
  • Handmade Fans: Hangzhou has a long history of fan-making, and these intricately designed accessories make excellent gifts or keepsakes.
  • Preserved Snacks: Local delicacies such as spiced nuts, preserved fruits, and Chinese pastries can be found in abundance here.

Hefang Street also features traditional medicine shops, including those specializing in Chinese herbal remedies, and craftsmen who demonstrate their skills in making calligraphy brushes or engraving seals.

2. Wulin Square and the Hangzhou Tower Shopping Center

If you’re looking for a more modern shopping experience, Wulin Square and the Hangzhou Tower Shopping Center are excellent choices. These locations cater to a more upscale audience but also offer high-quality souvenirs.

What to Look For:

  • Luxury Silk Products: The Hangzhou Tower has several stores specializing in premium silk products, including bedding, clothing, and accessories.
  • Tea Sets: Beautifully crafted ceramic and porcelain tea sets are perfect companions to the Longjing tea you’ll purchase elsewhere.
  • Contemporary Gifts: Modern art pieces and designer-inspired souvenirs are also available for those looking for something unique.

Wulin Square is also home to seasonal pop-up markets and events, making it a dynamic spot to explore.

3. China National Silk Museum

For those interested in the history and art of silk, the China National Silk Museum is not only a fascinating attraction but also a great place to shop for authentic and high-quality silk products.

What to Look For:

  • Exclusive Silk Items: The museum’s gift shop sells unique silk products, including scarves, wall hangings, and ties, often inspired by the exhibits.
  • Educational Value: The staff here are well-informed and can guide you in choosing items that represent the best of Hangzhou’s silk tradition.

The museum’s location near West Lake makes it an easy addition to your sightseeing itinerary.

4. Tea Plantations in Longjing Village

For the freshest Longjing tea, a visit to the tea plantations in Longjing Village is a must. This area is located in the lush hills surrounding Hangzhou and offers a chance to see how the tea is grown and processed.

What to Look For:

  • Fresh Longjing Tea: Many farmers sell directly to visitors, ensuring authenticity and freshness.
  • Tea Accessories: In addition to the tea itself, you can purchase tea canisters, strainers, and brewing tools.
  • Farm Tours: Some plantations offer guided tours and tea-picking experiences, which make for unforgettable memories.

Shopping here not only supports local farmers but also guarantees the quality of the tea you’re buying.

5. Qinghefang Old Street

Qinghefang Old Street is another historic shopping area that offers a charming blend of old-world ambiance and modern convenience. This street is a treasure trove for those seeking traditional Chinese crafts and souvenirs.

What to Look For:

  • Chinese Calligraphy Supplies: Shops selling brushes, ink stones, and paper are abundant here, and you might even find artists creating custom works on the spot.
  • Lacquerware and Ceramics: Beautifully designed items such as bowls, vases, and plates are available.
  • Traditional Clothing: Tang suits, qipao, and other traditional garments make unique and memorable gifts.

The street also has plenty of snack vendors and tea shops where you can relax and enjoy the local flavors.

6. West Lake Cultural Square

West Lake Cultural Square is a hub for art and culture in Hangzhou, featuring galleries, bookstores, and boutique shops. This area is ideal for those who appreciate artistic and literary souvenirs.

What to Look For:

  • Art Prints and Paintings: Many local artists sell works inspired by Hangzhou’s landscapes and landmarks.
  • Books and Stationery: Chinese poetry collections, notebooks with traditional designs, and handcrafted bookmarks are thoughtful gifts.
  • Cultural Trinkets: Small items like keychains and magnets featuring West Lake or other iconic Hangzhou scenes are perfect for casual souvenirs.

The square often hosts cultural events, so you might even find limited-edition items or artisan goods during your visit.

7. Silk Markets

In addition to the museum, Hangzhou has several silk markets where you can find an extensive range of silk products. These markets cater to both locals and tourists, offering everything from raw fabric to finished garments.

What to Look For:

  • Custom Tailoring: Some shops offer on-site tailoring services, allowing you to have silk clothing made to your specifications.
  • Home Décor: Silk pillowcases, curtains, and table runners add a touch of elegance to any home.
  • Affordable Options: While high-end silk is available, many markets also have budget-friendly options that maintain good quality.

Shopping at these markets is an excellent way to immerse yourself in Hangzhou’s silk heritage.

8. Local Markets and Night Bazaars

For a more eclectic shopping experience, consider visiting local markets and night bazaars. These lively spots are great for finding unique, inexpensive items and enjoying Hangzhou’s vibrant street culture.

What to Look For:

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Many vendors sell earrings, necklaces, and bracelets made from jade, wood, or other natural materials.
  • Traditional Toys: Wooden puzzles, spinning tops, and other nostalgic items are available.
  • Street Food: While not a traditional souvenir, local snacks and condiments can be taken home for a taste of Hangzhou.

Markets like the Wushan Night Market are especially popular and provide a fun, bustling atmosphere.

9. Hangzhou Arts and Crafts Museum

This museum not only showcases traditional Chinese arts and crafts but also has a well-curated gift shop where you can purchase handmade items.

What to Look For:

  • Embroidery: Delicate embroidery pieces, framed or as part of textiles, make beautiful decorative items.
  • Bamboo Crafts: Items like baskets, fans, and coasters highlight the skill of local artisans.
  • Workshops: The museum often hosts workshops where visitors can create their own crafts, adding a personal touch to their souvenirs.

The museum is a quieter alternative to the busier shopping streets, ideal for those looking for a more relaxed experience.

10. Online Options for Convenience

If you miss out on shopping during your trip, many Hangzhou-based stores offer online shopping platforms that ship internationally. This allows you to purchase authentic souvenirs even after you’ve left the city.

What to Look For:

  • Verified Sellers: Look for shops with good reviews and certifications to ensure quality.
  • Custom Orders: Some online stores allow you to request personalized items, such as monogrammed silk products or custom tea blends.

Shopping for souvenirs in Hangzhou is more than just a transaction; it’s an opportunity to immerse yourself in the city’s culture and history. Whether you’re browsing bustling markets, strolling through historic streets, or exploring modern shopping centers, Hangzhou offers countless ways to bring a piece of its charm back home.
