How to Plan the Perfect 5-Day Trip to Hangzhou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China’s most picturesque cities. Known for its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a blend of nature, history, and modernity. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a returning traveler, this 5-day itinerary will help you experience the best of Hangzhou.

Day 1: Exploring West Lake and Its Surroundings

Morning: A Leisurely Stroll Around West Lake

Start your trip with a visit to West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Rent a bike or take a leisurely walk along the lakeside, stopping at iconic spots like:
- Leifeng Pagoda: Climb to the top for panoramic views of the lake.
- Broken Bridge (Duan Qiao): Famous for its role in the legend of the White Snake.
- Bai Causeway: A scenic path lined with willow trees.

Afternoon: Lunch and Lingyin Temple

Head to Louwailou Restaurant for authentic Hangzhou cuisine, such as Dongpo Pork and West Lake Vinegar Fish. After lunch, visit Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat), one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples. Don’t miss the nearby Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar), adorned with ancient rock carvings.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

End your day with the "Impression West Lake" light and music show directed by Zhang Yimou. This outdoor performance, set on the lake, combines traditional Chinese culture with stunning visual effects.

Day 2: Tea Culture and Scenic Villages

Morning: Longjing Tea Plantations

Hangzhou is famous for Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, one of China’s finest green teas. Visit the Meijiawu Tea Village or Longjing Village to:
- Tour tea plantations.
- Learn about tea-picking and processing.
- Enjoy a traditional tea ceremony.

Afternoon: Lunch and Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies

Have lunch at a local farmhouse, then hike the Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies (Jiu Xi Shi Ba Jian) trail. This scenic route winds through bamboo forests, streams, and tea fields, offering a peaceful escape from the city.

Evening: Hefang Street

Spend your evening at Hefang Street, a historic pedestrian street filled with:
- Traditional handicraft shops.
- Street food stalls (try stuffed glutinous rice balls or beggar’s chicken).
- Cultural performances like puppet shows.

Day 3: Historical Hangzhou and Grand Canal

Morning: China National Tea Museum

Start your day at the China National Tea Museum, where you’ll deepen your understanding of tea culture through interactive exhibits and beautiful gardens.

Afternoon: Lunch and Gongchen Bridge

Enjoy lunch at Grandma’s Kitchen (Wai Po Jia), then explore the Hangzhou section of the Grand Canal. Key stops include:
- Gongchen Bridge: A historic landmark.
- Umbrella Museum: Showcasing traditional Chinese umbrellas.
- Canal boat ride: A relaxing way to see the waterfront scenery.

Evening: Xixi Wetland Park

If time allows, visit Xixi Wetland Park, a vast network of waterways and wetlands. Take a boat tour to see traditional houses and lush landscapes.

Day 4: Day Trip to Qiandao Lake

Full-Day Excursion to Qiandao Lake

Take a day trip to Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake), about 2.5 hours from Hangzhou. Highlights include:
- Boat tours to explore the lake’s islands.
- Adventure activities like zip-lining and kayaking.
- Local seafood for lunch.

Return to Hangzhou in the evening and relax at your hotel.

Day 5: Modern Hangzhou and Shopping

Morning: Hangzhou’s Tech Hub

Visit Alibaba’s headquarters (if you’re a tech enthusiast) or explore Xixi National Wetland Park if you missed it earlier.

Afternoon: Lunch and Shopping at In77

Have lunch at Green Tea Restaurant, then shop at In77, Hangzhou’s premier shopping district, featuring luxury brands, local designers, and trendy cafes.

Evening: Sunset at Baochu Pagoda

End your trip with a visit to Baochu Pagoda for a breathtaking sunset view over the city.

Additional Tips for Your Hangzhou Trip

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Cherry blossoms and pleasant weather.
  • Autumn (September-November): Cool temperatures and golden foliage.

Transportation

  • Metro: Efficient and easy to navigate.
  • Didi (ride-hailing): Convenient for short trips.
  • Bikes: Great for exploring West Lake.

Must-Try Foods

  • Dongpo Pork (braised pork belly).
  • West Lake Vinegar Fish.
  • Longjing Tea Shrimp.

With this itinerary, you’ll experience the perfect balance of nature, culture, and modernity in Hangzhou. Pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey!
