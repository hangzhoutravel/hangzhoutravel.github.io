Hangzhou in 5 Days: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China’s most picturesque cities. Known for its serene West Lake, lush tea plantations, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a blend of nature, history, and modernity. This 5-day itinerary will guide you through the best of Hangzhou, from iconic landmarks to hidden gems.

Day 1: Exploring West Lake and Its Surroundings

Morning: West Lake Scenic Area

Start your Hangzhou adventure at West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Rent a bike or take a leisurely stroll along the lake’s causeways, such as Su Causeway or Bai Causeway, which offer stunning views of willow trees, lotus flowers, and distant pagodas. Don’t miss the Leifeng Pagoda, which provides a panoramic view of the lake.

Afternoon: Lingyin Temple and Feilai Feng

Head to Lingyin Temple (Temple of the Soul’s Retreat), one of China’s most significant Buddhist temples. Explore its grand halls and intricate carvings before wandering through Feilai Feng (Peak Flown from Afar), a grotto filled with ancient Buddhist rock carvings.

Evening: Hefang Street

Wrap up your day at Hefang Street, a lively pedestrian street lined with traditional shops, teahouses, and snack stalls. Try local delicacies like Dongpo Pork and Beggar’s Chicken, or pick up souvenirs like silk scarves and Longjing tea.

Day 2: Tea Culture and Nature Escapes

Morning: Meijiawu Tea Village

Experience Hangzhou’s famed tea culture in Meijiawu, a charming village in the Longjing tea region. Tour a tea plantation, learn about tea-picking, and enjoy a freshly brewed cup of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea while soaking in the tranquil mountain views.

Afternoon: Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies

Hike through Nine Creeks and Eighteen Gullies (Jiuxi Shibajian), a scenic trail shaded by bamboo groves and dotted with streams. The peaceful atmosphere makes it a perfect escape from the city’s hustle.

Evening: Impression West Lake Show

End your day with the Impression West Lake, a breathtaking outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou. Set on the lake itself, the show combines lights, music, and dance to tell romantic legends associated with West Lake.

Day 3: Historical Hangzhou and Modern Marvels

Morning: China National Tea Museum

Dive deeper into tea history at the China National Tea Museum, where interactive exhibits showcase the evolution of tea culture in China. The museum’s serene gardens are ideal for a quiet morning stroll.

Afternoon: Six Harmonies Pagoda and Qiantang River

Visit the Six Harmonies Pagoda (Liuhe Pagoda), an ancient tower offering spectacular views of the Qiantang River. If you’re visiting during the tidal bore season (usually around September), witness the powerful Qiantang River Tidal Bore, a natural phenomenon.

Evening: Qianjiang New Town

Explore Hangzhou’s modern side in Qianjiang New Town, where skyscrapers like the Hangzhou International Conference Center light up the night. Enjoy dinner at a rooftop restaurant with a skyline view.

Day 4: Day Trip to Xixi Wetland or Qiandao Lake

Option 1: Xixi National Wetland Park

For a nature-filled day, visit Xixi Wetland, a vast network of waterways, marshes, and traditional villages. Take a boat ride, spot local wildlife, and visit ancient ancestral halls tucked among the reeds.

Option 2: Qiandao Lake (Thousand Island Lake)

If you prefer a longer excursion, head to Qiandao Lake, a stunning man-made lake with over 1,000 islands. Activities include island-hopping, zip-lining, and visiting the underwater ancient city ruins.

Evening: Return to Hangzhou

After your day trip, unwind at a lakeside café or indulge in a Hangzhou-style hot pot for dinner.

Day 5: Art, Shopping, and Farewell to Hangzhou

Morning: China Academy of Art (Xiangshan Campus)

Art lovers shouldn’t miss the China Academy of Art’s Xiangshan Campus, designed by architect Wang Shu. The campus blends modern aesthetics with traditional Chinese elements, making it a visual masterpiece.

Afternoon: Shopping at In77 or Wulin Square

Spend your final afternoon shopping at In77, Hangzhou’s premier shopping district, or Wulin Square, where luxury brands and local boutiques coexist. Pick up some Hangzhou silk or handmade fans as souvenirs.

Evening: Sunset at Baochu Pagoda

Before leaving, hike up to Baochu Pagoda for a farewell sunset over West Lake. The view is a perfect way to reflect on your Hangzhou journey.

This 5-day itinerary ensures you experience the best of Hangzhou—its natural beauty, cultural depth, and modern charm. Whether you’re sipping tea in a misty plantation or marveling at ancient temples, Hangzhou promises memories that linger long after you’ve left.
