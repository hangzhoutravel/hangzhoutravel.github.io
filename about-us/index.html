Hey, I’m Jake Morgan — a 32-year-old finance professional currently living and working in the dynamic city of Hangzhou, China. Originally from San Diego, California, I’ve always been drawn to places that offer both energy and calm — and Hangzhou fits that balance perfectly. It’s a city where business ambition meets lakeside serenity, and for someone like me, who thrives on both numbers and nature, it’s the ideal place to call home.


I came to China five years ago, initially for a short-term assignment with a global investment firm. What was supposed to be a 12-month rotation turned into something much bigger — not just a job, but a lifestyle I genuinely enjoy. Hangzhou captivated me from the start: the high-speed development, the tech-forward energy (being this close to Alibaba HQ is no joke), and of course, the stunning natural scenery. I’ve worked in fast-paced environments before — New York, Hong Kong — but Hangzhou has a unique rhythm. It’s ambitious, yet grounded.


In my current role, I work as a portfolio strategist, helping clients navigate cross-border investment opportunities, with a particular focus on emerging tech and ESG (environmental, social, and governance) markets. Finance is a competitive field, no doubt — but I enjoy the challenge. There’s a deep satisfaction in helping people grow their wealth responsibly. I’m analytical by nature, but I’m also a firm believer that people matter more than numbers. That mindset has helped me build not just a client base, but real relationships.


But life’s not all about spreadsheets and market reports. Outside the office, I’m a passionate traveler, fitness enthusiast, and photography hobbyist. Hangzhou is a dream base for weekend getaways. When I need to unwind, I hop on my motorbike and ride out to Moganshan — the bamboo forests there are a whole different world. On slower weekends, you’ll find me walking around West Lake at sunrise, coffee in hand, camera slung over my shoulder. The reflections on the water, the quiet early-morning mist — it’s the kind of peace you don’t get in many cities.


I’m also big on short trips. Whether it’s exploring the ancient water towns of Wuzhen and Xitang, hiking through Thousand-Island Lake, or taking a train down to Ningbo for some fresh seafood, I make it a point to leave the city at least twice a month. Travel fuels me — not just for relaxation, but for perspective. It reminds me that there's always more to see, more to learn, more to appreciate.


Fitness plays a big role in my routine too. I hit the gym five times a week — not because I’m chasing some impossible physique, but because discipline matters to me. A strong body supports a sharp mind, especially in a demanding industry like finance. I’ve also joined a local dragon boat team — it started as a fun social idea but turned into something I take seriously. There's something uniquely fulfilling about training with a team, syncing your strokes on the water at sunset.


One of the things I’ve really come to appreciate about Hangzhou is its lifestyle. It’s modern but not overwhelming, fast-paced but not exhausting. People here enjoy life — they go hiking, they take tea seriously, they sit by the river at night with friends and just talk. That kind of work-life integration is something I never fully experienced in the West. It’s not just “work hard, play hard” — it’s a smoother, more sustainable rhythm. I’ve adapted to that. I’d even say I’ve embraced it.


I’ve also been brushing up on my Mandarin — not just for daily life, but out of respect. Living in China has taught me that language is more than communication — it’s connection. Whether I’m ordering xiaolongbao at a local shop or discussing market trends with a colleague, being able to express myself in Chinese opens doors — culturally, professionally, and personally.


Looking forward, I see Hangzhou as more than just a chapter in my career — it’s part of my long-term story. I’m exploring the idea of launching a boutique investment consultancy that bridges Western capital and Chinese innovation. I want to stay grounded in the work I love, but with more flexibility to travel, mentor younger professionals, and maybe even teach part-time down the road.


To sum it up: I’m Jake Morgan — finance guy with a passion for movement, meaningful work, and meaningful moments. I believe in taking life seriously, but not too seriously. Whether I’m deep in market analytics or knee-deep in a mountain trail, I bring energy, focus, and an open mind. That’s the life I’ve built here in Hangzhou — and I’m just getting started.
